## [2015-04-23 12:39] eso

1. Briefly explain your role at ZOS
I'm the lead UI designer for the Elder Scrolls Online.
I created the visual language and designed the underlying systems to support a unified (and fun) console experience.
I work with other game designers, artists, engineers and QA to implement and evaluate gameplay features.


2. Why is the UI for console so different from PC?
People tend to play console games in a different environment than PC games, and of course the input devices are hugely different. We designed the interface to feel natural with a gamepad. When I'm on a PC, I'm 1 foot away from thescreen. I have numerous input methods available - at least 100 seperate buttons on the keyboard, and numerous inputs from the mouse. Over time I find myself slouching forward in my chair and sort of hovering over the control surfaces on my desk.

 In contrast to that, players tend to adopt a more relaxed posture in front of a game console and TV set. They play the game from a distance. The gamepad itself is a remarkable input device, but unlike a mouse and keyboard, there tends to be a travel time associated with navigation. For example, drag and drop is something you might take for granted with a mouse and keyboard. Its a very natural thing to do. Its surprisingly difficult to pull off with a game controller. The equivalent interaction for "take this thing and put it in some other place" (which we do a lot of) requires an alternate approach.

Potentially. We develop on PCs with gamepad support, however that's not quite yet ready for general purpose usage

We don't make any assumptions about different languages over VOIP.
