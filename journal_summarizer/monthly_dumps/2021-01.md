ESO IN 2021
Due to a variety of issues, including work from home, a very aggressive development schedule, chronic understaffing, and lack of QA resources, ESO's quality has slipped alarmingly. This cannot be allowed to continue in 2021 - additional staff has been approved to help shore up our dev team so we can work smarter and better and be able to fix long-standing issues and ensure that we do not introduce additional problems. With this in mind, we are purposefully focusing on bugs and performance issues over new features next year. We will have a couple of hallmark new features in 2021 (Companions, revamped Champion Point system), but other than those, we need to take the time and make the game perform better and not be so unstable.

The ESO BMT is working on a plan to greatly increase ESO's profile in the industry next year, with a campaign focused around Project Osprey (ESO next-gen) launch, E3, and our 2021 Chapter, Blackwood. This moment in time, in June, hopefully with a big stage presence, should go a long way to raise the media and industry's perception of ESO. We have already filmed the "virtual 2021 announce" show for our Gates of Oblivion content, and of course you all hopefully saw the amazing teaser trailer we debuted at The Game Awards a few days ago. 2021 will be a big year for us, and we are always well underway with plans to support it.

I have had a couple of opportunities to get a hands-on demo of Osprey running, and it is very cool. Essentially it is like ESO on a high-end gaming PC, but on a console. It looks and feels amazing. All three next-gen platforms will have user-selectable "Fidelity" and "Performance" settings so they can choose whether they would like a higher-fidelity or a higher-framerate experience.

SPG IN 2021
 We now have a functioning technical proof-of-concept for the ESO Companion App, and we've already approved the next set of features to be supported by it, as well as the next in-app game to help the Companion App gain some traction. I'm looking forward to see both substantially evolve over the next year and get the app in the hands of employees for testing.


2021 HIRING
We managed to hire over 50 new FTEs in 2020, even with the many hurdles the year threw at us. In 2021, we'll be ramping that up significantly, as we grow the Kestrel team in anticipation of production, and of course to support ESO with the much-needed staff it needs to keep it on the rails and moving forward. The hiring plans are aggressive, but it is nothing we haven't done before. By the end of 2021, we should have ESO staffed for (more) long-term success, and be well on the road to getting Kestrel into production in June 2022. Additionally, we’ll be getting our satellite studios off the ground in 2021, proving the concept, and moving forward.

Differences between Kanban & Scrum

TIMIMG
- Scrum
  + In scrum methodology teams prioritize workd and commit to a certain number of tasks within a sprint. Work is released at the end of each sprint
- Kanban
  + Kanban is not limited to an iteration or sprint cycle. It supports continuous delivery while maximizing

WORK IN PROGRESS
- Scrum
  + Tasks cannot be added to the scrum board in the middle of the sprint, but it is possible for all tasks to be in the In Progress column at the same time.
- Kanban
  + The team agrees on the number of tasks that can be in progress at the same time. As tasks are completed, team members pull new tasks from a queue.

CHANGE
- Scrum
  + Work is not added during a sprint
- Kanban
  + Work can be added at any time
