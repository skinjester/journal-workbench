## [2020-05-07 16:45] eso

From Max's notes during design review

Went over some work with Jason last week
Came away with discussions with Jared about how we can incorporate the cybernetics to get it represented somehow

- Looked at the idea of treating cybernetics on treating them as "dynamic slots" which didn't quite work
- Ended up modifying the layout to support a more central/hero based focus - taking some cues from skyrim or dragon age
	- In that both of those are less about slots and more about the items themselves

Touring the newest designs (see most recent documentation on hvault)
	- Includes a player status block
	- Features two weapon slots, an amp slot, and four armor slots
	- Treating everything as though they are similar objects - setting some conventions and running with them.
	- Cognizant of the fact that players like seeing the items on their character - which you can currently only do from the loadouts screen - considering if we want to incorporate it into the lower screens somehow

- Explored what it looks like if you have different amps running on the same platform (e.g.- your cybernetic arm can run either armstrong assault or scout drone, for example)

- Showed dedicated "comparison of items" modal screen

FEEDBACK
Landing page wound up in a great spot
- The properties you boiled down to are successful
- Dave wants to spend a little bit of time thinking about the flow for viewing and changing mods - though the parity of how the mods/abilities/etc is successful
- Concerns of whether or not there's enough of a connection between the equipment list and the mod list (particularly for the low items)
	- We could potentially solve this via a fixed focal point
	- Or use a dedicated "configure" launch point
	- This process might be a bit shaky
	- This gap probably won't be as much of an issue for armor, since there aren't customizable elements of armor

- It would be useful to highlight the relevant ammo type when you're focusing on specific weapons
	- Perhaps take it one step further and incorporate the ammo count somewhere more prominently?

- It would indeed be nice to get the player character on the left side of the screen - in the open space?
	- That would work well, from the standpoint of reinforcing that the loadout is to the left
	- Plus we could pan over to the character we selecting loadout

Looking good!
- The standardization is really successful
- Mods will be much more common than weapons as drops
- A future pass of this screen (not this round) will look at cosmetics.
- We'll need to have a meeting about this - determining what the player can do from the field vs. what they need to do to a vendor to take care of.
