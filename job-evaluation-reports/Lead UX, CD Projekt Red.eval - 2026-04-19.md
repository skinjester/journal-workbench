### PART 1 — What the role is really asking for

#### The explicit asks
- Lead UX for a new multiplayer RPG project, with ownership of visual, interactive, and workflow design.
- Build a player-centered experience across menus, navigation, game flow, and moment-to-moment clarity.
- Work cross-departmentally with UI art, research, production, and design leadership.
- Define inclusive best practices, guide other UX designers, and keep documentation actionable.
- Ship within a studio environment that expects AAA-level craft, communication, and implementation discipline.

#### The implied asks
- Translate fuzzy game design into a coherent system that can survive production pressure.
- Be comfortable in playtests, critique loops, and iterative tuning from observed player behavior.
- Help the team converge on a usable direction without over-designing or freezing the pipeline.
- Keep the experience readable on controller-first interfaces and large-screen living-room viewing.
- Operate as a lead who can mentor, align, and clarify, not just hand off polished screens.

#### What "great" looks like for this role
- The person can turn a complex RPG surface into something legible, welcoming, and shippable.
- They have enough game UX depth to speak to information architecture, navigation, and interaction rhythm without needing translation.
- They can show receipts for playtest-informed iteration and production-minded documentation.
- They improve team throughput by making decisions easier to review, implement, and maintain.
- They read as a studio lead who can collaborate with research, production, and art without friction.

#### What a hiring team is optimizing for in screening
- A lead who has shipped at least one AAA title and can survive a skeptical game-UX screen.
- Evidence of multiplayer or systems-heavy game thinking, even if that is not the only thing on the page.
- Clear proof that the candidate can work across disciplines and explain decisions cleanly.
- Signs that they can do the job in the studio context, not just talk about design craft in the abstract.

#### Likely doubts unless proven otherwise
- Whether the strongest proof is current enough for a new AAA production rather than only legacy shipping work.
- Whether the candidate has enough modern Unreal-adjacent or current-game context for the team’s tooling and taste.
- Whether the broader resume reads as game UX lead or as a more general systems/product designer.
- Whether Boston-based hybrid work is actually a fit, since the page does not prove location readiness.
- Assumption note: I am treating `shipped at least one AAA title` as a real screen gate. The Unreal line reads more like a useful capability signal than a hard requirement, while the multiplayer line is in `Nice to Haves` even though the project itself is multiplayer.
