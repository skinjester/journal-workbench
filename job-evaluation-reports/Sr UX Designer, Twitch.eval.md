### PART 1 — What the role is really asking for
#### The explicit asks
Twitch is asking for a senior IC who can work inside a live, cross-platform product with real community stakes. The explicit work is:

- partner closely with PM, engineering, research, and other designers
- turn streamer/viewer feedback into usable product direction
- design for content discovery and content engagement
- improve existing patterns instead of inventing an entirely new design language every time
- present clearly in reviews and feedback sessions
- mentor junior designers and raise team standards
- use both qual and quant signals to form hypotheses
- show strong Figma fluency and enough craft to make confident decisions

The role is not just visual polish. It is program-level UX leadership for streamer creation and viewer engagement.

#### The implied asks
The hidden asks are more important than the bullet list:

- understand creator economics and community behavior
- design for habits, not one-off tasks
- make engagement loops feel natural rather than manipulative
- balance novelty with pattern consistency across surfaces
- work comfortably across mobile and desktop, because Twitch is a multi-surface product
- communicate well upward, because the role reports into design leadership and may be shown to executives
- know when to simplify and when to let depth stay visible

This is a role for someone who can design a system that helps communities keep living, not just a screen that looks finished.

#### What "great" looks like for this role
Great here means a designer who can:

- translate messy creator/viewer problems into a clear product frame
- ship work that improves engagement without breaking trust
- show enough systems discipline that patterns scale across surfaces
- justify decisions with evidence, not just taste
- move comfortably from early concept to critique to implementation
- mentor without becoming abstract or hand-wavy

The best candidate will look part product designer, part systems thinker, part community product strategist.

#### What a hiring team is optimizing for in screening
They are likely trying to answer four questions fast:

1. Can this person design for live engagement, not only static flows?
2. Can they operate credibly in a cross-functional product environment?
3. Do they have enough craft and judgment to be trusted at senior level?
4. Can they make Twitch-specific problems feel native instead of transplanted from another domain?

#### Likely doubts unless proven otherwise
The likely concerns are:

- your public story is still game-heavy, not creator-platform native
- Twitch wants content discovery and engagement, but your public proof leans more toward game UX systems than social feed or creator tooling
- mobile prototyping is asked for explicitly, but not foregrounded in the most visible materials
- research is mentioned, but there is not much public evidence of deep recent customer research
- Figma is present, but the portfolio is doing more of the heavy lifting than the resume

That does not make you a bad fit. It just means the screening team may need to bridge the gap from games/live service to Twitch/live communities.
