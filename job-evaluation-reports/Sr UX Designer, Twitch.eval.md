## PART 1 — What the role is really asking for
#### The explicit asks
- Senior UX ownership for streamer creation and viewer engagement across Twitch's product ecosystem.
- Cross-functional collaboration with product managers, engineers, researchers, and other designers.
- Program-level influence: present work, defend decisions, and help move the roadmap.
- Hands-on research and hypothesis building from qualitative and quantitative signals.
- Strong interaction design, visual craft, Figma fluency, and enough prototyping skill to make ideas concrete.
- Mentoring and raising the bar for junior designers and design practice.
- Bonus signals around AI workflows, UGC/content-creator familiarity, complex research, and design systems.

#### The implied asks
- Translate messy community behavior into scalable product patterns.
- Design for both creator-side and viewer-side needs without losing the thread.
- Be comfortable in an ambiguous, fast-moving live product where the work is more about shaping the right problem than polishing a fixed brief.
- Make decisions with taste and confidence, then explain them in a way that PMs, engineers, and design leadership can use.
- Read as a senior product designer who understands systems, not a one-off visual designer.

#### What "great" looks like for this role
- A designer who can make Twitch feel clearer and more human at scale, not just prettier.
- Someone who can connect engagement, discovery, and community needs to concrete interaction patterns.
- A senior IC who can lead a program, mentor others, and still produce sharp hands-on work.
- A candidate whose public work shows both craft and judgment, with enough proof to trust the move from ambiguity to implementation.

#### What a hiring team is optimizing for in screening
- Fast bucketability as a senior product/platform UX designer.
- Visible overlap with content discovery, content engagement, and cross-platform service design.
- Evidence of collaboration, critique, research, and systems thinking.
- Confidence that the candidate can mentor and present without heavy scaffolding.

#### Likely doubts unless proven otherwise
- The public package does not explicitly show Twitch-native streamer tools, viewer-engagement loops, or UGC/community design.
- Most of the strongest proof is game/live-service adjacent, so a skeptical reader has to translate it into Twitch.
- Mobile prototyping and roadmap influence are present only implicitly or in smaller pockets, not front-and-center.
- Assumption: Twitch's bonus points are truly bonus. The screen is more likely to care about content discovery/engagement, cross-functional collaboration, Figma, prototyping, and senior judgment than about the optional AI/UGC bullets.
