## PART 1 — What the role is really asking for

#### The explicit asks

- Own the overall UX for a multiplayer Witcher-universe RPG.
- Design visual, interactive, and workflow UX for UI navigation, game flow, and moment-to-moment clarity.
- Lead and support a small UX team while working cross-departmentally with UI art, UX research, production, and design leadership.
- Prototype layouts, navigation structures, and usability systems.
- Run and interpret playtests, then turn player feedback into design changes.
- Produce and maintain documentation that production can actually use.
- Work in Unreal, or at least be comfortable moving into Unreal-adjacent prototyping.

#### The implied asks

- Be the person who can make a dense game readable without flattening it.
- Have enough taste to make tradeoffs without waiting for perfect certainty.
- Know how to turn research, community feedback, and production constraints into shipped UX.
- Be able to set standards and methodologies, not just make screens.
- Bring current game literacy, not generic product UX instincts.

#### What "great" looks like for this role

- A lead who can define a UX direction and keep it coherent across UI art, engineering, research, production, and design.
- Someone who has shipped AAA game UX before, ideally with multiplayer or live-service complexity.
- A designer who prototypes early, tests with players, and revises quickly when evidence contradicts the first idea.
- Clear documentation habits, good verbal and written communication, and enough presence to guide other UX designers.

#### What a hiring team is optimizing for in screening

- Fast proof that you have shipped AAA game UX, not just general product design.
- Evidence that you understand controller-first navigation, menus, onboarding, combat-adjacent flow, and accessibility.
- Signs that you can function as a hands-on lead in a cross-disciplinary game team.
- Confidence that you can work in a collaborative, iterative, and feedback-heavy production environment.

#### Likely doubts unless proven otherwise

- Whether your strongest proof is recent enough for a 2026 AAA role.
- Whether you have current multiplayer game UX instincts, not just historical MMO / console experience.
- Whether you are comfortable enough in Unreal and current prototyping workflows to contribute immediately.
- Whether this is a true lead profile or a very strong senior IC profile dressed up as leadership.
