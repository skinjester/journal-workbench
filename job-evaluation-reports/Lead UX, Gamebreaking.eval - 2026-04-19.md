### PART 1 — What the role is really asking for

#### The explicit asks

Gamebreaking is not hiring a narrow UI production pair of hands. They want a senior games UX operator who can take ownership of complex player-facing problems inside an embedded AAA co-dev setup, move quickly from wireframes into prototypes, and help turn those designs into scalable UI standards.

The posting is also unusually specific about tool-and-medium expectations. The strongest visible asks are: many years in games UX, comfort collaborating with leadership and production, standards/documentation discipline, coaching ability, and direct familiarity with Unreal-driven game UI. The diegetic UI / 3D-layout line matters here; it reads less like boilerplate than a real preference for someone who can think beyond flat menu flows.

#### The implied asks

Being the "first UX Designer at Gamebreaking" implies they want someone who can create order without much supervision. That means not just designing features, but defining process, critique norms, artifact quality, and how UX interfaces with design, engineering, production, and possibly client-side UX peers.

Because the role starts embedded with a large AAA partner, they are probably screening for someone who can integrate into an existing production machine without territorial behavior. They need a designer who can influence patterns and push quality while still respecting another studio's ecosystem and pipelines.

#### What "great" looks like for this role

Great here looks like a designer who can:

- own ambiguous feature spaces without waiting for perfect requirements
- translate game complexity into controller-friendly or spatially legible interaction models
- produce artifacts other disciplines can build from quickly
- set standards that survive scale, iteration, and multiple contributors
- give feedback with enough authority to improve the work, but without becoming socially expensive

The ideal candidate likely has recent, visible Unreal-native proof, shipped AAA game UX, and a portfolio that shows both systems thinking and craft detail.

#### What a hiring team is optimizing for in screening

On the first pass, they are probably optimizing for three things:

- obvious games credibility in seconds
- evidence of lead-level ownership, not just execution
- confidence that the person can raise UX quality on a small team without needing a lot of management

They are also likely watching for whether "lead" means true standards-setting influence or just a senior IC title from older credits.

#### Likely doubts unless proven otherwise

- How current is the candidate's game UX proof relative to today's Unreal-heavy pipelines?
- Is the leadership proof mainly craft leadership and critique, or direct coaching/directing of other designers in a small-team environment?
- Does the package prove diegetic UI and 3D-layout depth specifically, or mostly strong menu/system UX adjacent to that space?
- Is the candidate still a crisp games-UX fit, or has the public narrative drifted toward broader product/design-systems/creative-tech work?
