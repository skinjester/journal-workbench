## PART 1 — What the role is really asking for

#### The explicit asks

- Own the design vision for Coupa Supplier Experience foundations.
- Simplify onboarding, identity, profiles, navigation, and shared platform capabilities.
- Make Grow, Transact, and the broader portal feel coherent and trustworthy.
- Lead cross-functional work with Product, Engineering, Operations, research, and Design Systems.
- Produce concrete flows, prototypes, specs, and service maps.
- Help define how AI should support discovery, guidance, and next-best-action behavior.

#### The implied asks

- Be the person who turns fragmented supplier surfaces into one legible system.
- Reduce duplication and confusion across products, states, and roles.
- Make tradeoffs that balance trust, scalability, and implementation reality.
- Work comfortably in ambiguity without waiting for perfect requirements.
- Set a standard others can reuse instead of solving each problem in isolation.

#### What "great" looks like for this role

- A lead who can frame the platform problem, not just polish screens.
- Someone who can translate complex business and technical constraints into a durable UX strategy.
- Strong instincts for trust, identity, permissions, hierarchy, and cross-product flow.
- Evidence of design-system contribution and consistency at scale.
- Clear influence with engineering and product, plus enough craft to set the bar.

#### What a hiring team is optimizing for in screening

- Fast confidence that the candidate can lead a platform UX problem, not only a feature.
- Proof of simplifying complex workflows across multiple surfaces or user types.
- Signals that the candidate can work with systems, data, trust, and long-lived workflows.
- Readability for a recruiter who needs to bucket the person quickly into senior platform UX.
- Enough craft and collaboration evidence to survive deeper interview rounds.

#### Likely doubts unless proven otherwise

- The package is not obviously B2B SaaS or supplier-portal native.
- The strongest public proof is legacy game/platform work, not a recent enterprise product.
- AI shows up more as adjacent creative-tech work than as product platform work.
- Leadership is strong on influence and standards, but not obviously formal people management.
- The JD wants a Coupa-specific platform story that is not directly visible yet.
