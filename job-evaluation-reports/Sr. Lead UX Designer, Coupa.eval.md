## PART 1 — What the role is really asking for

#### The explicit asks

Coupa is hiring someone to own the design direction for a platform foundation layer: supplier onboarding, identity, profiles, navigation, shared patterns, and the connective tissue between products. The job also asks for cross-functional leadership, Figma-level execution, research collaboration, and clear design-system contribution.

#### The implied asks

This is not just “make screens nicer.” The role is about reducing fragmentation, making the supplier experience feel trustworthy, and turning platform complexity into something coherent across Grow, Transact, and the broader network. It also implies a lot of stakeholder alignment, roadmap shaping, and tradeoff management where design has to hold up under technical and organizational constraints.

#### What "great" looks like for this role

Great here means someone who can define a long-range foundation strategy and still produce crisp flows, prototypes, and component logic that engineering can actually build. A strong candidate should make the supplier journey feel legible, scalable, and consistent while also being able to simplify messy edge cases without flattening the product.

#### What a hiring team is optimizing for in screening

They will likely screen for platform thinking, trust-sensitive workflow experience, design-system maturity, and evidence that the designer can lead across PM/ENG/Operations without losing clarity. They also want proof that the candidate can translate ambiguity into a coherent roadmap and that the story on the page matches the story in the portfolio.

#### Likely doubts unless proven otherwise

The main doubt is domain translation: this is B2B SaaS / supplier-network work, while much of the candidate’s strongest public proof is in games and adjacent platform web work. A second doubt is AI: the JD mentions AI-enabled platform experiences, but the public package needs to show whether that is a product-design strength or only a thematic adjacency. The resume and portfolio partly mitigate both concerns through identity, onboarding, navigation, and shared-systems work, but they do not fully erase them.
