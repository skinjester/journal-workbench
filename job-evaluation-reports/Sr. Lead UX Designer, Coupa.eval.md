### PART 1 — What the role is really asking for

#### The explicit asks

- **Sr. Lead IC design ownership** for the **Foundations** pillar of Coupa’s **supplier** experience: **onboarding, identity, profiles, navigation**, and shared capabilities that let suppliers move across **Grow / Transact** (and the broader network) without fragmentation.
- **Consolidate toward a single “Coupa Supplier Portal” (CPS)** mental model—reduce duplication, establish trust and clarity, and make Coupa legible as one network rather than a pile of products.
- **Platform + systems UX**: coherent patterns as complexity scales; explicit partnership with **Product, Engineering, Operations**; roadmap shaping and **tradeoff advocacy** (Foundations is called out as non-revenue but organizationally critical).
- **AI-enabled foundational experiences**: guided onboarding, profile intelligence, proactive recommendations—**not** a decorative AI slide; the role expects you to help **define how AI shows up** in supplier-critical flows.
- **Hands-on execution at a high bar**: flows, components, prototypes, interaction specs, **service maps**; **Figma** and **mature design systems** are table stakes.
- **Research leadership / collaboration** with **global suppliers**—onboarding friction, trust signals, cross-product friction translated into **platform-level** improvements.
- **Design system contribution** in partnership with a centralized DS team—patterns that **scale web and mobile** and stay consistent across product lines.
- **Experience bar**: **10+ years** in **complex B2B SaaS, platform, or marketplace** contexts with multiple products/integrations.

#### The implied asks

- Political skill: Foundations work is **invisible when good and blamed when bad**; success is measured in **reduced support burden, completion rates, time-to-value, and cross-product coherence**—not a flashy launch metric.
- Ability to **sequence** a multi-year consolidation (single portal narrative) without boiling the ocean—**incremental coherence** that still reads as one vision.
- Comfort with **procurement-domain complexity** (supplier/buyer asymmetry, permissions, org hierarchies, compliance) even when the JD soft-sells the domain.
- **Operational reality**: suppliers are not a monolith; edge cases and exceptions are the job.

#### What "great" looks like for this role

- A **single narrative** for “how a supplier enters and lives inside Coupa” that product, eng, and ops can execute against, with **evidence-backed** prioritization (research + metrics).
- **Shipped** improvements to onboarding/identity/navigation within the first two quarters that show up in **adoption and task success**, not only Figma polish.
- **Patterns** that other teams adopt because they reduce rework—**the Foundations pillar becomes the default place** for shared supplier primitives.
- Credible **AI** stories: assistive, explainable, and **safe** in high-stakes account contexts—not generic copilots.

#### What a hiring team is optimizing for in screening

- **B2B / platform / marketplace** keywords and titles that **survive ATS and recruiter keyword gates** (“B2B SaaS,” “platform,” “design system,” “onboarding,” “identity”).
- **10+ years** with **recent** evidence of **senior scope** (strategy + craft + influence), not only execution.
- **Figma + design systems** as non-negotiable hygiene signals.
- **Supplier-side empathy** framed as **trust, clarity, and task completion** under complexity.

#### Likely doubts unless proven otherwise

- **Domain**: Entertainment/games reads as a **different planet** from procurement spend management unless you **translate** platform and account work into B2B language fast.
- **Title / level**: “Senior UX Designer” on the resume vs **Sr. Lead** at Coupa—screeners may **under-level or mis-bucket** before the HM sees nuance.
- **Research**: Game/user research is not the same as **global B2B supplier** research; expect skepticism unless you show **transferable methods and outcomes**.
- **AI**: Interactive art/ML demos impress creatives; enterprise buyers want **governance, accuracy, and failure modes**—portfolio must be **framed**, not left to assumption.
