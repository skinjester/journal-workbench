## PART 1 — What the role is really asking for

#### The explicit asks

Eleventh Hour wants a **senior game UX designer** who **owns** player-facing structure for **gameplay systems, menus, and commerce** on a live, complex RPG. The role is **product-minded UX** (wireframes, flows, IA, Figma components, prototypes, documentation), not UI art or engineering, but with enough engine/production literacy to design within real constraints.

Hard signals in the text: **5+ years of shipped game UX**, **expert Figma** (components, variables, auto layout, prototyping), **gamepad + mouse/keyboard** literacy, **console cert awareness**, **RPG systems + in-game shop** experience, and **cross-functional** partnership with design, UI art, engineering, QA, and production. They also call out **Figma component library ownership** explicitly.

#### The implied asks

This is a **reduce ambiguity for the team** role: make systems legible early, standardize patterns, and keep implementation from becoming one-off band-aids. The commerce line means **monetization surfaces** need to feel native to the RPG fantasy, not bolted on.

The studio profile (ARPG, **Last Epoch**, live-service speed) implies comfort with **dense progression/inventory/shop** mental models, iteration from **playtest and cert feedback**, and the political skill to **champion a design system** without becoming a blocker.

#### What "great" looks like for this role

Someone who can show **receipts** for: systems-heavy shipped UI, **controller-first** reasoning, **shop and live-service** adjacent work, and **tooling** (Figma library thinking) that matches how modern teams build. "AAA-level UX mindset" here reads as: taste + production tradeoffs, not a literal AAA studio badge.

#### What a hiring team is optimizing for in screening

- Fast bucket: "senior **game** UX" with **shipped RPG-ish complexity** and **Figma systems** depth.
- Proof of **owning** flows end-to-end (not only supporting UI art).
- Minimize **figma resume inflation**: they will look for **component/variables/prototyping** credibility in the portfolio and interview stories.

#### Likely doubts unless proven otherwise

- **Figma expert** is a high bar: screeners will match claim to case-study evidence.
- **ARPG** is a preference, not a strict gate; the doubt is "do we get **action-RPG / modern RPG** fluency" vs general UI skill.
- **Recency and contract framing**: a skeptical reader may ask whether the most recent work maps to the **same** senior IC ownership model as an in-house seat.
