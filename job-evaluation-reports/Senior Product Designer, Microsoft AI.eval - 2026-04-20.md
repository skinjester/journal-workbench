# PART 1 — What the role is really asking for

#### The explicit asks

Microsoft AI is hiring an IC product designer who can define core interaction patterns for Copilot across capabilities, not just polish isolated screens. The visible asks are high craft, prototyping ability, end-to-end ownership, strong cross-functional execution, comfort with experiments and measurement, and enough product judgment to translate business goals into UX and technical requirements.

This is also a consumer role, not an enterprise systems role. They want someone whose work feels emotionally legible, polished, fast, and trustworthy in a product category where behavior, tone, and micro-interactions matter as much as flows.

#### The implied asks

- They want a designer who can work like a startup IC inside a major company: self-directed, fast, low-drama, and implementation-close.
- They want someone comfortable shaping ambiguous behavior in a product that is part assistant, part interface, and part brand relationship.
- "Always be earning trust" and the references to safety, security, and evaluation imply they care about failure modes, not just delight.
- The mention of AI model training teams implies product-design collaboration with technical partners whose work changes system behavior, not just UI surfaces.

#### What "great" looks like for this role

Great here likely means a designer who can make AI feel useful, calm, and coherent at the interaction-pattern level; prototype quickly; pressure-test ideas with research and metrics; and hold a high visual/interaction bar through implementation. The strongest candidates will show recent consumer product work where ambiguous intelligence had to be made legible and trustworthy.

#### What a hiring team is optimizing for in screening

- Immediate readability as a senior consumer product designer, not a specialist from an adjacent lane.
- Direct proof of prototyping and interaction design craft.
- Evidence of owning a product area across requirements, experiments, launch, and iteration.
- Confidence that the candidate can partner with engineering and data-minded teams without slowing the product down.
- Signals that AI curiosity is real and practical, not just trend awareness.

#### Likely doubts unless proven otherwise

- "Is this mostly a games designer with adjacent product language?"
- "Where is the direct proof of recent consumer software launches outside games?"
- "Where is the evidence of designing with AI-specific trust, safety, and behavior constraints?"
- "Has this person actually worked in an experiment-and-metrics loop for a consumer app, or mostly in systems/craft roles?"
- "Will the portfolio read as product design for Copilot, or as strong but adjacent interaction craft?"
