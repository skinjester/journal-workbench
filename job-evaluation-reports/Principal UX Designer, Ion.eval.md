### PART 1 — What the role is really asking for

#### The explicit asks

- **Principal-level UX leadership** on **enterprise** desktop, web, and mobile work for **external clients** (consultancy delivery, not a single in-house product org).
- Own **UX strategy and execution**: workflows, layouts, interactions, and **data visualizations** for complex, **data-driven** applications—especially in **financial services** contexts.
- Run or direct **user research**, synthesize findings, and ship **actionable** design outcomes (wireframes, prototypes, testing).
- **Client-facing**: workshops, presentations, iterative refinement with stakeholders.
- **Mentor** other UX designers and set quality bars for the practice.
- Partner tightly with **engineering**, **product**, and **visual design** so designs actually land.
- **10+ years** in UX / IxD / IA; degree in HCI or adjacent field listed as required in the posting.

#### The implied asks

- Comfort operating where **business constraints**, **technical reality**, and **user needs** conflict—weekly, in rooms with people who own budgets and roadmaps.
- Credibility in **regulated, high-stakes** domains (money movement, institutional workflows); ability to speak the client’s language without a long ramp.
- **Consulting stamina**: context-switching across accounts, proof-of-value fast, and surviving scrutiny from client-side SMEs.
- **On-site presence in Boston 3–4 days/week** plus possible travel—this is a geographic and lifestyle filter as much as a collaboration preference.

#### What "great" looks like for this role

- A Principal who can **frame** a messy enterprise problem, **defend** a UX strategy under skeptical technical and business review, and **delegate/elevate** a team without becoming a deck-only figurehead.
- Someone whose portfolio and references make **financial/enterprise complexity** feel familiar—dense tables, permissions, exceptions, audit trails, and instrumented workflows—not just marketing-site polish.

#### What a hiring team is optimizing for in screening

- **Title and trajectory**: “Principal” + ~10+ years + evidence of **leading** UX on hard systems (not only shipping UI).
- **Domain adjacency**: asset management, trading, portfolio management, institutional tooling—**preferred** but often used as a tie-breaker among Principals.
- **Consulting DNA**: client references, agency/consultancy tenure, or long embedded engagements with measurable outcomes.
- **Local fit**: Boston on-site expectation screens out most fully remote West Coast profiles unless they are unusually strong or willing to relocate.

#### Likely doubts unless proven otherwise

- **Industry**: AAA games and live-service entertainment are not fintech; the burden is to show **transferable** systems thinking, not assume automatic respect for the domain shift.
- **Role archetype**: The resume reads **strong senior IC / lead** in games and embedded studio work; “Principal” in **enterprise consulting** is a different political and narrative job.
- **Education line**: The JD lists a **Bachelor’s/Master’s in HCI or related** as required; your resume’s education mix may trip strict ATS or HR checks even if a hiring manager would waive it.
- **Location**: San Francisco + remote-friendly history vs. **Boston-heavy on-site** is a serious mismatch unless relocation is on the table.
