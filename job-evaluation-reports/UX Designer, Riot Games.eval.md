## PART 1 — What the role is really asking for

#### The explicit asks
- Design and optimize player-facing UX in a high-scope, ambiguous game area.
- Produce wireframes, prototypes, presentations, and other artifacts that let the team review and build the work.
- Work closely with design, engineering, art, and project leadership to move from concept to implementation.
- Articulate rationale clearly and use data, research, or player insight to support decisions.
- Help set quality standards and user-centered design practices for the team.

#### The implied asks
- Be comfortable in early R&D where the problem is not fully defined yet.
- Handle multiple features and cross-team continuity without losing the player story.
- Know how to work with game input, platform constraints, and implementation realities.
- Bring enough breadth to cover interaction design, systems thinking, research, and presentation quality.
- Have a personal relationship with games and enough design intuition that player empathy feels real, not borrowed.

#### What "great" looks like for this role
- A senior game UX designer who can turn ambiguity into a clear structure quickly.
- Someone who can prototype, explain, and iterate without needing the problem to be fully solved first.
- A designer who keeps the player experience coherent across features, surfaces, and teams.
- A person whose work reads as both practical and player-centered, not just visually polished.

#### What a hiring team is optimizing for in screening
- Fast bucketability as a senior game UX designer with real shipped experience.
- Evidence that you can work across disciplines and keep momentum in an ambiguous product area.
- Proof that you can make design decisions legible to non-designers and help them build the work.
- Signals that you understand players, not just interface mechanics.
- A believable fit for an unpublished R&D product where the team needs structure, not only ideas.

#### Likely doubts unless proven otherwise
- The JD asks for deep mobile game experience, and the public package does not strongly prove that. I am treating that as a potential preferred qualification unless the team says it is a hard gate.
- The JD mentions strategy games; the portfolio shows broad game depth, but not a dedicated strategy-game thesis.
- The strongest shipped game proof is not recent. That is still credible for this role, but it may prompt a recency question.
- The games resume is strong on systems and console/MMO work, so a skeptic may wonder whether the candidate is broad enough for an R&D product area rather than only legacy game systems.
