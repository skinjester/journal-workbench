## PART 1 — What the role is really asking for

#### The explicit asks

Riot is hiring a senior-level game UX designer who can work across multiple features in an ambiguous R&D environment, not just polish isolated screens. The role asks for hands-on flow design, wireframes, prototypes, presentations, and enough implementation partnership to prove ideas out with engineering and UI support.

The posting also makes cross-functional communication central. This is not a quiet craft role. They want someone who can explain rationale, socialize player-first decisions with design, engineering, art, and leadership, and help establish quality bars and UX practice for the team.

#### The implied asks

The hidden ask is product definition under uncertainty. "Unpublished R&D product," "high-scope," and "often ambiguous" imply a team still converging on systems, feature boundaries, and even the right artifacts for decision-making. They want someone who can impose structure without needing the game to be fully known first.

The role also implies systems thinking across touch points. "Experiential continuity across platforms and touch points" suggests account, progression, navigation, social, or service-adjacent interactions may matter alongside core feature UX. It is broader than HUD polish.

#### What "great" looks like for this role

Great here looks like a designer who can turn fuzzy game concepts into coherent player journeys, prototype enough behavior to get useful critique, and then translate that work into artifacts a team can actually build from. Strong candidates will show both feature-level craft and architecture-level judgment.

For this particular posting, "great" probably also includes game-native instincts: player empathy, comfort with mechanics and systems UX, ability to think across platforms, and enough collaborative gravity to shape how an R&D team works, not just what one flow looks like.

#### What a hiring team is optimizing for in screening

- Clear senior game UX signal in the first 20 seconds.
- Portfolio proof of systems thinking, interaction design, and prototype-driven exploration.
- Evidence of ambiguity handling, cross-discipline influence, and implementation awareness.
- A credible player-first story, not just polished visual design.
- Bonus signals: mobile, strategy-game affinity, recent multi-device UI work, and explicit Figma-era workflow comfort.

#### Likely doubts unless proven otherwise

- Is the candidate current enough for an R&D game team, or is the strongest public proof too concentrated in older console/live-service work?
- Does the package prove feature definition in ambiguous preproduction, or mostly shipped systems from earlier roles?
- Can this person work in modern multi-device/mobile contexts, or is that mostly adjacent?
- Is the story tailored to a player-experience game role, or does it still lean too much toward broader systems/platform framing?
