### PART 1 — What the role is really asking for

#### The explicit asks

- Own **player-facing UX for mechanics and game features** in a **high-scope, ambiguous R&D** context: flows, wireframes, interactive prototypes, presentations, and **implementation-adjacent** proof (working with engineering/UI to prototype).
- **Cross-discipline fluency**: communicate to design, engineering, and art; drive **player-first** decisions without waiting for perfect clarity.
- **Full artifact stack**: from discovery/research framing through journey maps, flows, wireframes, mockups, and prototypes—plus **articulate rationale** and a **data-informed** story (however the team defines “data” here: telemetry, research, playtests, etc.).
- **Leadership without necessarily being a people manager**: help set **best practices and quality bars**, champion **user-centered methods**, and work with **project leadership** on standards.

#### The implied asks

- Comfort with **iteration under uncertainty** (R&D + “unpublished” means direction can shift; features may be exploratory).
- Political and communication skill: **align** across teams when your work **spans multiple features** and sometimes **multiple teams**.
- **Taste + rigor**: Riot’s copy emphasizes **delight** and **player empathy**—they want someone who can defend decisions without becoming brittle when challenged.
- **Tooling credibility**: Figma called out; prototyping culture is explicit.

#### What "great" looks for this role

- A senior IC who can **name tradeoffs**, show **progressive fidelity** (not just pretty mocks), and **ship learning**—prototypes that reduce ambiguity for the team, not just slides.
- Someone whose portfolio proves **game UX craft** *and* **systems thinking** (loops, edge cases, platform constraints), not only marketing-site work.
- Evidence of **influencing** quality standards (critique culture, documentation, patterns) in a multidisciplinary environment.

#### What a hiring team is optimizing for in screening

- **Portfolio first**: “inspiring professional portfolio” is required; they will look for **game-relevant** breadth (flows, UI systems, motion/feedback literacy, complexity).
- **Recency and relevance**: 5+ years is the floor; they will still ask whether your strongest proof looks **current** (tools, live service literacy, multi-platform).
- **Mobile + strategy** (desired): easy keyword screen—if the resume/portfolio does not surface **mobile game UX** or **strategy genre** interest, you may be filtered as “strong generalist, wrong specialization” unless the HM overrides.

#### Likely doubts unless proven otherwise

- **Mobile games depth** (desired, not required): your public proof skews **console/PC MMO**, **AAA console UI**, and **installation art**—not F2P mobile live ops.
- **Strategy passion** (desired): no obvious RTS/4X/moba-strategy portfolio thread; they may assume generic “likes games.”
- **Title alignment**: résumé top lines emphasize **Lead** / **platforms & systems**; screener may wonder if you will accept **UXD III** scope or push for **staff/principal** framing.
- **Founder/consultant years**: some HMs still discount contract work unless tied to **shipped** outcomes and **team scale**—your ZOS/EA anchors help, but Skinjester-era narrative must be crisp.
