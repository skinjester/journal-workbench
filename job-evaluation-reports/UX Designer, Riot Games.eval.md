### PART 1 — What the role is really asking for
#### The explicit asks
- Design player-facing UX for a complex, ambiguous R&D game product.
- Produce clear flows, wireframes, prototypes, and presentation artifacts.
- Collaborate tightly with design, engineering, art, and project leadership.
- Help implement prototypes well enough to prove the concept, not just describe it.
- Communicate design rationale with data-informed, player-first clarity.
- Bring broad UX coverage, with Figma and other prototyping tools expected.

#### The implied asks
- Be comfortable inventing structure where the feature space is still undefined.
- Work from concept through development without needing constant direction.
- Think beyond one screen: multi-feature continuity, multiple touch points, multiple teams.
- Have enough technical fluency to prototype and iterate in partnership with engineering.
- Bring real game instincts, not generic product UX dressed up as game taste.
- Have some adjacency to strategy-game thinking and strong player empathy.

#### What "great" looks like for this role
- A senior designer who can turn a messy mechanic or feature area into a coherent player journey fast.
- Someone who can make prototypes, defend tradeoffs, and keep the team aligned without turning the job into process theater.
- A portfolio that shows shipped or near-shipped game UX, not just pretty concept comps.
- Evidence of console/gamepad fluency, cross-platform thinking, and implementation awareness.
- Enough craft and leadership to improve standards across a team, not just a single feature.

#### What a hiring team is optimizing for in screening
- Can this person make an ambiguous game system legible and playable?
- Do they actually understand game production constraints, or only talk about them?
- Will they collaborate well with engineers and art, and keep the player experience first?
- Can they prototype quickly enough to move the team from idea to proof?
- Are they senior enough to handle scope, but still hands-on enough to deliver?

#### Likely doubts unless proven otherwise
- The strongest public game shipping proof is older, so recency needs care.
- The package does not clearly prove mobile-game depth, even though the JD explicitly likes it.
- Strategy-game passion is implied at best, not loudly surfaced.
- This reads as a lead-leaning IC, which may be stronger than the title band or what some teams expect from "Designer III."
- The R&D/unpublished product angle raises the bar for ambiguity tolerance, and the public package has to do all the work.
