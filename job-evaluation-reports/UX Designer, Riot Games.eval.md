### PART 1 — What the role is really asking for

This is a senior IC UX role for a game that is still being figured out. Riot is not asking for a production-line UI finisher. They want someone who can shape game-feature UX while systems are still moving, make ambiguity legible for other disciplines, and keep the player experience coherent across touch points.

**Responsibilities behind the wording**

- Own UX for broad, ambiguous, multi-feature problem spaces that may cut across teams.
- Produce whatever artifact moves the team forward: strategy framing, research synthesis, flows, journey maps, wireframes, prototypes, and presentations.
- Work close enough to engineering and UI implementation to help prove concepts, not just describe them.
- Influence standards and process quality without necessarily managing people.

**Capabilities: essential vs preferred vs implied**

- **Essential:** senior interaction design craft, strong game/product UX judgment, portfolio quality, collaboration across design/engineering/art, comfort with iteration, fluency in modern design tools.
- **Preferred:** breadth outside core UX specialty, multi-device thinking, mobile depth, strategy-game affinity, formal HCI/design background or equivalent proof.
- **Implied:** Riot wants someone who can survive critique, articulate rationale, and make incomplete systems legible to others.

**What “great” looks like for this level/title**

- A senior IC who reduces ambiguity for the team instead of waiting for perfect requirements.
- Someone who can connect game mechanics, surrounding systems, and player understanding into one coherent experience.
- A designer who can move from concept to prototype to implementation-facing discussion without losing the thread.

**What a hiring team is optimizing for in screening**

- Fast proof that this is a real game UX candidate, not a generic product designer trying to pivot into games.
- Evidence of R&D tolerance: prototyping, concept work, systems still in motion, partial information, and cross-team dependency.
- Enough player empathy and systems literacy to discuss behavior and experience, not just layouts.

**Likely doubts/risks for a candidate coming from your background unless proven otherwise**

- The clearest gap is **mobile games depth**. Riot asks for it explicitly and your strongest public proof is console/PC MMO, systems UI, and adjacent prototypes.
- There is no meaningful **strategy-game** signal in the materials reviewed.
- Your positioning splits between **platform/trust/service UX** and **game systems/live-service UX** depending on which resume is used. That creates avoidable screening friction.
