### PART 1 — What the role is really asking for

#### The explicit asks

Lab49 is hiring for a senior client-facing UX lead who can own complex enterprise product work end to end: strategy, workflows, layouts, interaction design, research, stakeholder presentations, and implementation partnership. The direct asks are not just "strong UX" in the abstract. They want someone who has already designed dense, data-driven software and can look credible in front of financial-services clients.

The title also matters. "Principal" here reads as a player-coach consultant: strong individual craft, but with enough authority to shape direction, mentor other designers, and influence product decisions without waiting for perfect clarity.

#### The implied asks

- A consultancy posture: fast problem-framing, fast credibility, and comfort presenting unfinished thinking to clients.
- Enough enterprise product literacy to structure workflows, information density, and data views without over-indexing on consumer polish.
- Research fluency that is practical, not academic: synthesize findings, turn them into decisions, and keep delivery moving.
- Calm cross-functional leadership with engineers, PMs, visual designers, and external stakeholders.

#### What "great" looks like for this role

Great for this JD is someone who can walk into a messy financial or operational workflow, understand the business and technical constraints quickly, simplify it without dumbing it down, and defend the design in front of skeptical stakeholders. They do not need to be only a finance specialist, but they do need visible proof that they can handle enterprise complexity, data density, and client-facing ambiguity at a principal level.

#### What a hiring team is optimizing for in screening

They are likely optimizing for four things in the first pass:

- obvious enterprise/product maturity
- clear proof of complex workflow design
- confidence in client-facing communication
- at least some credible proximity to financial, trading, or institutional-investor tooling

This is not a posting where generic "senior UX designer" experience will read as enough. The burden is on the application package to make the enterprise and stakeholder story legible fast.

#### Likely doubts unless proven otherwise

- Has he actually shipped enterprise software, or is the package mostly games plus creative tech?
- Can he handle financial workflows and data visualization, or would the team be betting on transferability?
- Is the client-facing consulting muscle real and recent, or only inferable from adjacent materials?
- Does the package prove principal-level UX strategy and research leadership, or mostly lead IC execution on complex systems?
