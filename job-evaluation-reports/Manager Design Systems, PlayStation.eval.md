## PART 1 — What the role is really asking for

#### The explicit asks
- Lead a design systems team across multiple PlayStation consumer products and platforms.
- Own UI components, design tokens, documentation, contribution practices, and governance.
- Manage backlog, system debt, roadmap priorities, and adoption.
- Partner with Product and Engineering to ship production-ready patterns.
- Coach and develop designers.

#### The implied asks
- Be the person who keeps a large system coherent while still allowing product teams to move.
- Translate ambiguity into structure, rules, and clear review paths.
- Balance craft, consistency, accessibility, and speed.
- Evangelize process, standards, and reuse without becoming a blocker.
- Navigate cross-team and cross-regional friction without losing the long-term architecture.

#### What "great" looks like for this role
- Clear proof of design systems at scale, not just pretty UI or one-off product work.
- Real people leadership, or at minimum obvious design leadership with mentoring and review discipline.
- Evidence of governance: contribution rules, lifecycle management, quality review, and adoption follow-through.
- Confidence with complex multi-platform product surfaces and accessibility constraints.
- A candidate who can talk to Product, Engineering, and Design without sounding like they belong to only one of those groups.

#### What a hiring team is optimizing for in screening
- Can this person run a living system, not just design screens?
- Can they make tradeoffs without creating chaos?
- Can they lead designers and keep the bar consistent?
- Do they understand the realities of implementation, reuse, and maintenance?
- Will they sound credible in a room full of product, engineering, and design leaders?

#### Likely doubts unless proven otherwise
- No explicit people-management track record on the resume.
- No explicit adoption metrics, governance operating model, or component lifecycle ownership in public materials.
- The strongest proof is game UX and legacy design system work, which can read as adjacent rather than current product-design-systems management.
- Accessibility is referenced in the JD, but it is not a strong public headline in the materials reviewed.
- The public story reads more like senior/staff systems IC than a proven manager of a design-systems org.
