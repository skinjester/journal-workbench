### PART 1 — What the role is really asking for

#### The explicit asks

This is a hands-on design systems management role with three layers of work:

- Own the quality and evolution of UI components, design tokens, and system documentation across platforms.
- Keep Figma libraries usable, contribution paths clear, and component lifecycle governance stable.
- Reduce design debt, triage backlog, and partner with Product and Engineering so the system is actually adopted.
- Guide and coach designers while maintaining craft standards and accountability.
- Work across a messy multi-product ecosystem, including cross-team and cross-region coordination.

#### The implied asks

The posting is also asking for someone who can do more than maintain a library. It wants a person who can turn a big, horizontal product surface into a coherent operating system for design.

That implies:

- judgment about when to standardize and when to allow product-specific variation
- enough technical fluency to work with engineering without hand-waving
- enough process discipline to keep a system sustainable over time
- enough people leadership to create clarity without over-controlling the team
- enough taste and confidence to keep the system moving forward, not just frozen in place

Because this is a PlayStation role, I am treating the game/consumer-product context as a real preference, but not every line in the posting should be read as equally strict. Accessibility, global collaboration, and adoption strategy look like important requirements. Some of the broader brand language reads like standard Sony boilerplate, so I weight the operational lines more heavily than the marketing copy.

#### What "great" looks like for this role

Great here means the design system is not a side artifact. It is a living product with:

- reusable components and patterns that teams actually trust
- documentation that reduces ambiguity instead of adding it
- governance that keeps quality high without grinding the org down
- measurable adoption or at least clear evidence that the system changed team behavior
- a manager who can coach designers, not just polish assets

At this level, "great" also means the candidate can explain tradeoffs cleanly to Product and Engineering, keep multiple workstreams moving, and still sweat the details.

#### What a hiring team is optimizing for in screening

The screener is likely asking:

- Can this person be bucketed immediately as a design systems lead/manager for a consumer or game platform?
- Have they actually built and governed systems at scale, or only contributed to them?
- Is there visible evidence of cross-functional influence, not just design craft?
- Do they look like someone who can lead designers and keep a system healthy over time?
- Do they have enough accessibility, documentation, and platform fluency to make the role feel low-risk?

For a first-pass screen, the team is probably optimizing for credible fit signal, not a perfect match on every line.

#### Likely doubts unless proven otherwise

The main doubts from this JD are straightforward:

- formal people management is implied, but not strongly demonstrated on the resume page
- accessibility is requested explicitly, but the public materials do not lead with it
- adoption strategy and measurement are requested, but not strongly quantified in the public package
- the strongest proof is older AAA and platform work, so the team may wonder how current the design-systems operating model is
- some public proof comes from game UX and creative R&D, which is adjacent but not identical to a modern design systems manager role

The mitigating counterpoint is that the resume and portfolio do show repeated system-building at scale, cross-studio coordination, and documentation discipline. That makes the doubts real, but not fatal.
