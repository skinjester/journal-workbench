## PART 1 — What the role is really asking for

#### The explicit asks

Coupa is not hiring for a feature designer. This is a platform-facing lead role for the supplier side of a multi-product ecosystem. The visible asks are end-to-end ownership of onboarding, identity, profile management, navigation, shared workflows, and platform conventions across Grow, Transact, and the broader network.

The role also expects a designer who can operate at three levels at once: set direction, produce hands-on flows and prototypes, and align product, engineering, operations, research, and design systems around shared patterns.

#### The implied asks

The unspoken problem is fragmentation. Coupa appears to have supplier experiences split across products, touchpoints, and states, and wants one person who can make those seams feel like one trusted portal instead of several stitched-together systems.

This is also a trust-and-identity job. "Single source of truth" means the team wants a designer who understands sign-in state, profile integrity, permissions/roles, wayfinding, and how a user knows where they are and what account context they are in.

The AI language reads as real but secondary. They likely want someone who can frame AI as assistive guidance inside onboarding, profile completion, and recommendations, not someone whose value rests on AI novelty alone.

#### What "great" looks like for this role

Great for this role looks like a senior systems designer who has already led foundational B2B SaaS or marketplace experiences where multiple products, identities, and workflows had to behave like one coherent platform. That person would show current proof of:

- simplifying ugly platform complexity into clear user journeys
- defining reusable patterns with a design systems team
- influencing roadmap and tradeoffs across functions
- grounding decisions in research with real customers
- shipping improvements that increased adoption, trust, or operational clarity

#### What a hiring team is optimizing for in screening

They are likely screening for immediate readability more than raw design talent. A recruiter or hiring manager will want to see "enterprise platform lead" fast: B2B SaaS, onboarding, identity, cross-product architecture, design systems, and platform stewardship. They are probably not looking to infer those capabilities from adjacent work if the package does not name them clearly.

They are also optimizing for low ambiguity around level. "Sr. Lead" here reads like a high-autonomy senior IC or staff-ish lead who can own a pillar without needing managerial authority to do the work.

#### Likely doubts unless proven otherwise

- Has this candidate actually led complex B2B SaaS or marketplace UX, or is the strongest proof mostly from games and adjacent systems work?
- Is there direct proof of identity, permissions, onboarding, and cross-product portal design in a current enterprise context?
- Can he show supplier/customer research and operational constraints beyond internal game-team collaboration?
- Does he read as a platform lead for web/mobile product ecosystems, or as a very good systems-minded game UX designer?
- Is the AI mention substantive product thinking, or only adjacent experimentation?
