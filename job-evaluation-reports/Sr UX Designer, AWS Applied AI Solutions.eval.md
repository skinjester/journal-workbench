## PART 1 — What the role is really asking for
#### The explicit asks
- Define end-to-end UX strategy for a complex, multi-service product area.
- Design AI-native experiences, especially proactive, context-aware AI teammates.
- Build trust, transparency, and human-AI collaboration patterns.
- Use system-level thinking to create reusable patterns, frameworks, and quality standards.
- Prototype quickly with AI-accelerated workflows and influence decisions through tangible artifacts.
- Partner with PM, engineering, and science; influence senior stakeholders.
- Use research, experimentation, and data to validate decisions.
- Mentor other designers and raise the design bar.

#### The implied asks
- Translate ambiguous problem spaces into a point of view, not just screens.
- Make abstract AI capability legible, safe, and useful in the first pass.
- Work in 0->1 or heavily unsettled product areas without needing a perfect brief.
- Hold product strategy and execution together without drifting into pure concept work.
- Be comfortable in Amazon-style scrutiny: crisp rationale, receipts, and repeatable patterns.

#### What "great" looks like for this role
- A designer who can turn AI capability into an opinionated workflow, not a vague assistant blob.
- Someone who can establish interaction rules for trust and transparency before the product calcifies.
- Strong enough craft to build convincing prototypes, but structured enough to scale the pattern after the prototype.
- Clear storytelling that moves senior stakeholders from "interesting" to "we should build this."

#### What a hiring team is optimizing for in screening
- Fast bucketability as a senior product/system designer who can handle AI and complexity.
- Evidence of collaboration with product, engineering, and science, not solo design theater.
- Proof that you can simplify high-ambiguity surfaces without flattening them into generic UI.
- Signs you can prototype quickly and use artifacts to drive decisions.

#### Likely doubts unless proven otherwise
- Your strongest public work is game/platform heavy, not business-app or enterprise AI native.
- The public AI evidence is real, but older and closer to art/interaction experiments than current product AI.
- The resume language is broad enough to sound credible, but not yet sharp enough to scream "AWS Applied AI Solutions."
- There is no obvious recent proof of working directly with applied scientists on a trust-sensitive AI teammate product.
