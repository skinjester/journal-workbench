### PART 1 — What the role is really asking for

#### The explicit asks
- Own **end-to-end UX strategy** for **AI-native business applications** and multi-step customer journeys—not single widgets in isolation.
- Design for **AI teammates**: proactive, context-aware behavior, and **trust/transparency** patterns that scale across services.
- Operate as a **senior IC who leads**: opinionated directions from ambiguity, strong **interaction craft**, and **influence** on roadmap and senior stakeholders.
- **Partner with product, engineering, and applied science** (science is first-class here—not an afterthought).
- **Build to decide**: AI-accelerated workflows, rapid prototyping, and “vibe coding” to create artifacts that move alignment.
- **Research, experimentation, and data** as ongoing habits; **mentor** designers and raise collective quality.

#### The implied asks
- Comfort with **Amazon-style** ambiguity, scrutiny, and operational rigor (even if the JD does not spell every leadership principle).
- Ability to translate **model behavior + system constraints** into experiences people will trust in production—not demo gloss.
- Evidence you can **scale patterns** (frameworks, standards) across teams and surfaces, not only ship one beautiful flow.

#### What "great" looks like for this role
- A portfolio and interview narrative where **human–AI collaboration** is concrete: states, failures, recovery, escalation, explainability—not only visual polish.
- Stories where you **changed product bets** using artifacts and evidence (research, metrics, prototypes), especially across **multi-service** journeys.
- Demonstrated **taste + systems**: reusable models that reduce thrash as AI capabilities evolve.

#### What a hiring team is optimizing for in screening
- **7+ years** read as *credible senior scope* (ownership of complex systems, not only execution under tight briefs).
- **AI-related proof** (preferred) or a very strong adjacent story (ML-adjacent products, copilots, intelligent workflows) plus appetite to go deep fast.
- **Readability**: “Senior UX for applied AI in enterprise” in the first screen—not “game UI lead” unless reframed hard.

#### Likely doubts unless proven otherwise
- **Domain adjacency**: games and creative installations vs **B2B/enterprise business applications** at AWS scale.
- **Science partnership**: can you credibly work day-to-day with applied scientists on model limits, evals, and failure classes?
- **Recency and stack**: strongest public case studies skew **older shipped titles**; the JD wants **current AI-native product** instincts.
