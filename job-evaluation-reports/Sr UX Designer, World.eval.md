### PART 1 — What the role is really asking for

#### The explicit asks

- Own **end-to-end UX** for a **biometric iris-verification ecosystem**, spanning **the person being scanned** and the **operator** running verification in distributed deployments.
- Design across **physical, digital, audio, and environmental** touchpoints: OOBE, device surfaces and enclosure, stands/accessories, deployment context; plus on-device UI, remote/operator displays, mobile, web.
- Specify **multi-sensory** behaviors: **audio**, **lighting**, **haptics** / physical feedback—not only screens.
- Run **research** with participants, operators, and partners; translate to requirements, prototypes, flows, guidelines.
- Lead **NPI** (concept through launch) and **in-field sustaining** (iterate from real-world performance and feedback).

#### The implied asks

- Comfort operating where **hardware, firmware, industrial design, software, and operations** meet; ability to defend design intent through **build stages** and **manufacturing reality** (posting sits on Device team with supply chain / field ops flavor).
- Tolerance for **global, high-stakes, privacy-sensitive** identity UX: clarity, trust, error recovery, and consistency across cultures and environments.
- Evidence you can make **0-to-1 and 1-to-n** decisions under ambiguity (scaling principles across regions and profiles).

#### What "great" looks like for this role

- A **single coherent system story**: participant journey + operator journey + environmental context, with measurable clarity (what to do, what happens next, what failure looks like).
- **Operational credibility**: designs survive **field variance** (lighting, noise, crowding, training level) and **versioned hardware**.
- **Cross-functional pull**: research that changes requirements; prototypes that reduce disagreement; specs that manufacturing and firmware can execute.

#### What a hiring team is optimizing for in screening

- **Portfolio proof** of **multi-touchpoint** and preferably **hardware-adjacent** work—not only mobile/web UI.
- **Seniority**: 7+ years with evidence of **leading** ambiguous workstreams, not only executing tickets.
- **Title alignment**: posting title is **Senior UX Designer** on **Industrial Design Team**; body copy says **UX Design Lead** for the ecosystem—screeners may look for **leadership language** and scope.

#### Likely doubts unless proven otherwise

- **Domain**: Entertainment/game UX is a different buyer than **biometric hardware + global identity**; they will ask whether you can translate to **regulated, physical-world** deployments.
- **Industrial design partnership**: heavy collaboration with **ID and mechanical** is a plus, not clearly the center of gravity in your public narrative.
- **Recency**: strongest public case studies skew **games-era**; contract work is summarized but not always as inspectable as AAA pages.
