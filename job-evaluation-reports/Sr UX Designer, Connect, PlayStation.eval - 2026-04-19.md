### PART 1 — What the role is really asking for

#### The explicit asks

This is a senior platform UX role, not a general game feature role. The job is asking for someone who can own high-friction, high-trust journeys across account, identity, support, and safety surfaces, then carry those designs from concept through launch across console, web, and companion environments.

It also wants a designer who can turn technical and policy constraints into usable interaction models, contribute to shared standards, prototype through ambiguity, mentor other designers, and communicate clearly with product, engineering, legal, trust and safety, support, and regional stakeholders.

#### The implied asks

The team is probably not looking for pure visual craft or pure gameplay UX. They want someone who can handle platform UX where usability, compliance, privacy, abuse prevention, and business needs collide.

The repeated references to identity, safety, family management, support tools, localization, and global scale imply a service-design-fluent systems designer who is comfortable with messy ownership boundaries and cross-org dependencies. This is closer to platform trust/product infrastructure UX than to content-feature UX.

#### What "great" looks like for this role

Great here looks like a senior IC who can make a complicated service ecosystem feel coherent, safe, and low-friction. That means strong interaction design, clean system thinking, credible design-system instincts, and evidence of navigating ambiguity without waiting for everything to be defined first.

For this level, "great" probably also means the person can improve the team's operating model: tightening standards, mentoring, facilitating critiques, and helping product and engineering teams make cleaner decisions earlier.

#### What a hiring team is optimizing for in screening

- Direct evidence of account, identity, support, safety, or other trust-heavy platform work.
- Multi-surface shipping proof, especially console plus web/mobile/service surfaces.
- Clear systems thinking, not just isolated screen craft.
- Senior-level collaboration with technical and cross-functional partners.
- A portfolio that proves the candidate can simplify complex flows and define reusable patterns.

#### Likely doubts unless proven otherwise

- Is the candidate truly current on platform trust work, or mostly a games UI/system designer with adjacent experience?
- Can they prove direct work with privacy, safety, abuse mitigation, family management, or legal/compliance stakeholders, not just general complexity?
- Are the strongest proofs recent enough and visible enough in public materials?
- Does the package show enough measurable outcomes and launch-at-scale service UX, or mostly qualitative craft and systems narratives?
