## PART 1 — What the role is really asking for
#### The explicit asks
- Own the UX direction for a multiplayer RPG: menus, navigation, workflows, player journeys, and moment-to-moment interaction clarity.
- Partner tightly with UI art, research, production, and design leadership rather than operating as a lone wireframer.
- Lead UX layouts, interaction guidelines, prototypes, documentation, and playtest-informed iteration.
- Support and guide other UX designers while establishing inclusive methods and best practices.
- Arrive with shipped AAA credibility and enough game-literacy to make strong judgment calls quickly.

#### The implied asks
- This is a lead IC role with directional authority, not just a senior execution role.
- The team likely wants someone who can make order out of preproduction ambiguity before the game's UX hardens.
- They want a designer who understands controller, menu, and information architecture problems as game design problems, not only interface polish.
- "Inclusive, intuitive, immersive, rewarding" is partly values language, but it also implies practical accessibility and cognitive-load discipline.
- The Unreal line reads as useful implementation fluency, but not necessarily a requirement to be a production UI engineer.

#### What "great" looks like for this role
- A candidate who has already owned complex game UX across menus, systems, and player flow in a shipped AAA context.
- Someone who can turn research, feedback, and live play observations into clearer interaction rules and reusable patterns.
- A lead who can elevate a small UX team through critique, documentation, and standards without becoming bureaucratic.
- A portfolio that shows real systems thinking and design judgment, not just attractive screens.

#### What a hiring team is optimizing for in screening
- Fast recognition of "AAA game UX lead" rather than "strong generalist designer."
- Proof of multiplayer or live-service thinking, especially around navigation, progression, social, and commerce systems.
- Evidence of collaboration across design, art, engineering, QA, research, and production.
- Confidence that the person can prototype, test, and refine, not only describe a UX ideal.
- A story current enough that the team does not worry the candidate's best game proof is too far in the past.

#### Likely doubts unless proven otherwise
- Whether the strongest multiplayer AAA proof is current enough for a newly scoped lead role in 2026.
- Whether "lead" means formal team leadership here, while the package mostly shows lead-level influence and systems ownership.
- Whether Unreal experience is substantial enough to matter beyond prototyping.
- Whether the public package shows current genre awareness and present-day multiplayer taste.
- Whether Boston hybrid is viable.
