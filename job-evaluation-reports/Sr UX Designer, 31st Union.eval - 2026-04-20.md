## PART 1 — What the role is really asking for

#### The explicit asks

31st Union wants a senior game UX designer who can do more than wireframe menus. The role expects end-to-end ownership of complex interaction problems, from concept through in-engine implementation, with enough technical fluency to modify Blueprints and game data when needed.

The JD is also explicit about controller-era game UX specifics: diegetic UI, 3D layouts, interaction flow design, visual hierarchy, usability tuning in build, and documentation artifacts that help design, engineering, production, and leadership stay aligned.

#### The implied asks

This is not just a craft role. It is a trust role. They want someone who can absorb ambiguity, propose structure quickly, survive approval cycles, and keep implementation moving without needing constant producer intervention.

The phrase "player-first mindset" plus the emphasis on tuning usability in build suggests they want a designer who can notice friction in motion, not just present polished frames. The workflow language also implies a team that values designers who leave behind reusable methods, not just one-off feature work.

#### What "great" looks like for this role

Great here likely looks like a senior IC or lead-leaning IC who:

- translates messy feature ideas into controller-legible interaction models quickly
- is comfortable thinking in world space and 3D presentation, not only flat HUD/menu conventions
- can prototype or validate inside engine rather than stopping at static wireframes
- improves the team's operating system through standards, critique, documentation, and mentoring

#### What a hiring team is optimizing for in screening

On first pass, they are likely optimizing for obvious shipped game UX, engine fluency, and signs that the candidate has already worked through controller constraints, navigation complexity, and production handoff friction. They will also care whether the candidate reads as current enough for a senior role in active development rather than as a strong veteran whose best directly relevant proof is older.

#### Likely doubts unless proven otherwise

- How current is the strongest game-specific proof relative to a present-day senior game UX role?
- Is the candidate strongest in menus/systems/onboarding, or equally strong in diegetic and 3D spatial UX?
- Can they truly work in-engine at the level this team wants, or is the technical fluency mostly adjacent/prototyping-oriented?
- Does the application package tell one sharply targeted "senior game UX" story, or a broader systems/product/creative-tech story with games inside it?
