## PART 1 — What the role is really asking for
#### The explicit asks
- Own the full UX of a multiplayer RPG: visual, interactive, and workflow design.
- Work cross-department with UI art, research, production, and design direction.
- Lead and guide other UX designers while establishing best practices.
- Prototype layouts, navigation, and usability systems.
- Run playtests and convert player feedback into better flows.
- Document the work cleanly enough for production to implement.

#### The implied asks
- This is not a "make screens pretty" role. They want someone who can define structure and get teams aligned.
- The role is about player comprehension under constraints: menus, navigation, moment-to-moment clarity, and inclusive access.
- They want a lead who can translate vague preproduction into something implementable without losing intent.
- Multiplayer matters even though the JD does not make it the whole story. That implies complexity, live feedback, and edge-case pressure.

#### What "great" looks like for this role
- Shipped AAA game UX, ideally on a complex console or multiplayer title.
- Strong information architecture, not just UI polish.
- Can prototype fast in the tools and engines the team uses.
- Can hold a clear opinion, then adjust it based on research and playtest evidence.
- Can mentor and raise the quality bar across a team without becoming a bottleneck.

#### What a hiring team is optimizing for in screening
- Immediate AAA credibility.
- Evidence that you can lead, not just contribute.
- Signs that you can work with production realities and implementation details.
- Player-centered reasoning, especially around menus, journeys, and clarity.
- Someone who can show a coherent game UX story in under a minute.

#### Likely doubts unless proven otherwise
- The current resume opening reads more like systems/platform UX than lead game UX.
- Team leadership shows up as critique, standards, and mentoring, but not as explicit people management.
- Research/playtest leadership is present in the record, but not aggressively foregrounded.
- The strongest proof is older than ideal for a 2026 posting.
- If the screen is hunting for current multiplayer UX leadership, you will need the portfolio and journals to do extra work.
