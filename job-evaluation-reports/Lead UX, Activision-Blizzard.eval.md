## PART 1 — What the role is really asking for

#### The explicit asks

- **Own UX direction for CoD:Mobile** as the **first dedicated UX hire** on the product team: not just screens—**problem selection**, **direction setting**, and **repeatable practice** for a group that has been operating without a UX anchor.
- **Translate evidence into design artifacts**: player data, research, competitor analysis → **concepts, flows, wireframes, prototypes**, aligned with Product.
- **Remote partner management**: **set direction** with **Tencent** (their UX/design/production executes detail). This is vendor/partner **influence without direct report authority**.
- **Post-ship evaluation**: measure how UX changes perform and **feed learnings** back into the roadmap.
- **Process and standards**: **establish** how UX works inside the CODM team (templates, critique, handoff, measurement—whatever “best practices” means there).
- **Logistics**: **Santa Monica**, **four days onsite**; **evening meetings with China** (stated as roughly weekly-ish).

#### The implied asks

- **Political and narrative skill**: “first UX role” means you will hit **prior decisions, partner constraints, and turf**. You need **stakeholder management** as much as Figma skill.
- **Mobile F2P literacy**: monetization loops, seasonal content, performance on low-end devices, and **pace of live ops**—even if not spelled out in bullets, the product context implies it.
- **FPS/mobile shooter intuition**: understanding **COD’s** UX expectations (loadouts, flows, readability under stress). “Extra points” for playing CoD or similar is a soft filter with real hiring weight.
- **Comfort being the “adult in the room” on UX**: Senior Director of Product as manager suggests they want someone who can **represent UX credibly** to leadership and to Tencent.

#### What "great" looks like for this role

- A portfolio and interview story that shows **end-to-end UX leadership on a live game** (not only UI polish): **prioritization**, **measurement**, **shipping**, **iterating**.
- Proof you can **collaborate across companies and time zones** without owning the whole implementation—clear artifacts, crisp rationale, **disagree-and-commit** when the partner owns pixels.
- Evidence you have **stood up or upgraded UX practice** where it was thin (playbooks, reviews, standards).

#### What a hiring team is optimizing for in screening

- **Title and years**: “Lead” + **10+ years** is a hard gate; they will scan for **game/live product** and **mobile**.
- **Risk reduction on “first UX”**: they want someone who has **already done** “define how UX works here,” ideally in games, ideally with **analytics** and **ship cadence**.
- **Partner model fit**: résumé signals that read as **embedded with engineering/production** and **external studios** beat pure agency deck work.

#### Likely doubts unless proven otherwise

- **No Call of Duty and no mobile shooter headline** on the résumé—screeners may bucket you as **MMORPG/console + web commerce**, not **mobile FPS**.
- **Contractor / Skinjester** years may read as **less org-embedded** than a full-time lead at a publisher—even if the work was deep.
- **Location**: Bay Area vs **Santa Monica 4-day onsite** is a real friction point not visible on paper.
- **Mobile-first**: strongest public proof is **controller/console and PC MMO UX**; touch-native patterns are **less front-and-center** than for a typical mobile-first candidate.
