## PART 1 — What the role is really asking for

#### The explicit asks

This is not a pure craft-only UX seat. Activision is asking for a senior game UX lead who can identify problems worth solving, frame them for product leadership, and set direction for another team to execute in detail. The role owns concepts, flows, prototypes, and high-level UX direction across key systems and surfaces for `Call of Duty: Mobile`.

The hard requirements are clear enough:

- 10+ years in UX/product design or adjacent work
- mobile-first design experience
- evidence of improving player experience in a meaningful way
- strong communication and influence
- comfort working cross-functionally with PMs and with an external development partner

The soft-but-real asks are also important: player empathy, research fluency, and postlaunch iteration based on how changes actually perform.

#### The implied asks

Because this is the first dedicated UX role on the CODM product team, they are not just filling execution capacity. They want someone who can create operating leverage:

- give PMs a sharper way to define UX opportunity areas
- establish standards and process where none currently exist on the Activision side
- create enough clarity that Tencent can execute without constant re-interpretation
- bring product thinking, not just screen design

There is also an implied diplomacy requirement. The role sits between product leadership in Santa Monica and detailed design/implementation with Tencent, so the hiring team is likely screening for people who can influence without direct authority and keep direction coherent across org boundaries and time zones.

#### What "great" looks like for this role

Great here probably looks like a lead who can point to shipped live-service game systems, can talk fluently about player pain points and funnel friction, and can show that research, competitive analysis, telemetry, and design iteration all fed each other. For this specific team, "great" would be someone whose materials already show mobile/F2P instincts, postlaunch tuning, and comfort operating at product-team altitude rather than only embedded feature execution.

#### What a hiring team is optimizing for in screening

On first pass, they are likely optimizing for:

- obvious game UX credibility
- live-service systems experience
- product-partner fluency with PMs
- enough strategic seniority to define standards
- evidence the candidate can succeed in a cross-studio operating model

They are probably not looking for a generic app designer who likes games, but also not just a legacy console UI specialist. They want someone who can sit in the product loop of a large, active mobile game.

#### Likely doubts unless proven otherwise

- No visible shipped mobile game work in the submittable package.
- No direct free-to-play mobile economy or live-ops optimization proof called out by name.
- Research/data fluency is present only partially and inconsistently; it is not a dominant visible thread.
- The strongest shipped game proof is older AAA console/PC work, not recent mobile live service.
- Cross-studio collaboration is visible, but working with a major external development partner like Tencent is still adjacent rather than directly proven.
