## PART 1 — What the role is really asking for

#### The explicit asks
- Lead UX direction for key systems and surfaces in Call of Duty: Mobile.
- Turn player data, research, and competitor analysis into concepts, flows, wireframes, and prototypes.
- Work tightly with Product Management to choose the highest-impact UX problems.
- Coordinate with Tencent on implementation direction.
- Measure post-launch UX changes and feed iteration.
- Establish UX standards and process for a team that does not yet have a dedicated UX lead.

#### The implied asks
- This is as much a builder-of-practice role as a craft role: define the workflow, not just the screens.
- The team likely wants someone who can influence without authority and translate ambiguity into decision-ready artifacts.
- The first dedicated UX seat means the person will need to create structure quickly while staying lightweight enough for a live product.
- Because this is a game/AAA posting, I treat the mobile-first and CODM experience lines as important signals, but not every “extra point” as a hard must-have.

#### What "great" looks like for this role
- A senior UX lead who can read player pain, frame the right problem, and push the team toward a shippable answer.
- Someone who can balance research, data, and practical implementation constraints without getting stuck in abstract critique.
- A person who can partner with PMs and an external development partner, set standards, and keep iteration moving after launch.
- Someone who can show clear mobile-game intuition or at least a strong adjacent product-systems background that transfers cleanly.

#### What a hiring team is optimizing for in screening
- Fast proof of seniority and leadership, not just visual polish.
- Evidence that the candidate can operate in a live-service environment and stay useful after launch.
- Clear signs that the candidate can turn messy input into a coherent UX direction.
- Confidence that the candidate can work cross-functionally and communicate cleanly across product, design, and engineering.

#### Likely doubts unless proven otherwise
- The package needs to prove mobile-first thinking more directly than a console/PC-heavy background usually does.
- The JD asks for player data, research, and competitor analysis; a screener may look for visible evidence of that habit, not just broad UX skill.
- CODM and Tencent-specific collaboration are not visible in the materials, so that process fit remains an assumption.
- In a game/AAA posting, I assume the “extra points” language is mostly wishlist, while mobile-live-service fluency is closer to the core bar.
