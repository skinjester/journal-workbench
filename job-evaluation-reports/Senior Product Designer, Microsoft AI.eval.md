- Set a bar for excellent consumer product design in a fast-moving environment.
- Mentor and coach where useful, but this is still an IC role.

#### The implied asks
- Make AI feel simple, warm, and trustworthy instead of technical or cold.
- Prototype quickly and think in interaction patterns, not just screens.
- Navigate ambiguity and get work done despite blockers.
- Use research and data without turning the product into a metrics exercise.
- Be easy to work with: positive energy, empathy, kindness, and low drama.

#### What "great" looks like for this role
- A designer who can define the experience, not just decorate it.
- Someone who can turn fuzzy AI capability into clear, emotionally intelligent interactions.
- A craft bar that holds up at consumer scale: clarity, restraint, detail, and polish.
- Strong judgment about trust, safety, and when to keep things simple.
- Enough systems thinking to shape product patterns across the whole companion experience.

#### What a hiring team is optimizing for in screening
- Proof you can ship world-class consumer UX in a fast-moving environment.
- Evidence you can prototype, explain, and iterate with engineers and researchers.
- A signal that you understand trust and safety in AI product design.
- A person who looks immediately useful, not like someone who needs basic product-design training.

#### Likely doubts unless proven otherwise
- No explicit modern consumer AI shipping on the resume.
- The public story still reads game/platform-heavy rather than Copilot-native.
- The strongest proof is older, so recency will be questioned.
- No obvious LLM prompting or model-collaboration language on the application-facing resume.
