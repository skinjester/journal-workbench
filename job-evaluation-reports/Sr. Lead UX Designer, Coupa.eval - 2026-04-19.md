## PART 1 — What the role is really asking for

Coupa is not hiring for a feature-level UX generalist here. This reads like a senior lead IC who can impose product structure on a fragmented multi-product supplier ecosystem, especially around onboarding, identity, profile, navigation, and shared platform conventions.

#### The explicit asks

- Lead the design vision for the supplier-facing foundations layer: onboarding, identity, profile management, navigation, and shared workflows.
- Simplify complex, cross-product B2B SaaS experiences into something coherent and trustworthy.
- Partner tightly with product, engineering, and operations on roadmap tradeoffs, not just screens.
- Produce hands-on deliverables: flows, components, prototypes, specs, and service maps.
- Contribute to design-system evolution across web and mobile.
- Bring 10+ years of complex product design experience, ideally in B2B SaaS, marketplace, or multi-product environments.
- Show some credible angle on AI-enabled product behavior, especially guided onboarding, recommendations, or profile intelligence.

#### The implied asks

- Make a fragmented supplier ecosystem feel like one product, with the supplier portal acting as the control plane.
- Design trust-heavy admin moments well: account setup, identity, permissions-adjacent behavior, and cross-product continuity.
- Operate as a lead without hiding behind leadership language. They want direction-setter plus hands-on builder.
- Bring enough systems thinking to reason across business rules, data, workflow state, and organizational constraints.
- Help the org converge on platform standards, not just improve isolated flows.

#### What "great" looks like for this role

- Someone whose portfolio shows they have already untangled messy foundational workflows, not just polished end-user features.
- Someone who can explain how onboarding, identity, profile, and navigation fit together as one service surface.
- Someone who is fluent in design systems and platform conventions, but still strong enough in craft to prototype and specify hard interaction details.
- Someone who can credibly influence PM, engineering, ops, and design-system partners without needing formal management authority.

#### What a hiring team is optimizing for in screening

- Fast recognition of `platform / foundations / systems` fit.
- Clear evidence of designing high-stakes flows where trust, continuity, and data/state matter.
- Evidence that the candidate has worked in a multi-product environment, not only a single app or isolated feature pod.
- A resume and portfolio story that says "senior lead for complex product foundations" before it says anything else.
- Ideally, some visible B2B SaaS or marketplace proof so they do not have to make an adjacency leap.

#### Likely doubts unless proven otherwise

- "Is this mostly a games candidate, or a real fit for enterprise platform UX?"
- "Where is the strongest public proof of supplier/admin/identity/profile work in a modern SaaS context?"
- "Is the AI mention substantive, or mostly exploratory/adjacent?"
- "Can this candidate translate systems skill into enterprise workflow and operational constraints, not only game or experiential contexts?"
- "Is this a lead IC for foundations, or a broad creative systems designer whose story still needs tightening?"
