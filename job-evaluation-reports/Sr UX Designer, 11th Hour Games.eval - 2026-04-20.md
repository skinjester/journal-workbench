## PART 1 — What the role is really asking for

#### The explicit asks

This is a senior game UX systems role, not a general product-design opening and not a UI art seat. Eleventh Hour wants someone who can take messy feature requirements and turn them into implementation-ready game UX across gameplay systems, menus, and commerce.

The hard requirements are visible: shipped game UX experience, strong portfolio receipts, expert Figma usage, comfort with both gamepad and mouse/keyboard, console-constraint awareness, and the ability to work cross-functionally with design, UI art, engineering, QA, and production.

#### The implied asks

They are hiring for someone who can reduce ambiguity for the rest of the team. "Wireframes, flows, handoff documentation, versatile Figma components and prototypes" translates to: make the system legible early, standardize patterns, and save engineering and art from inventing structure mid-production.

The commerce mention matters. This is not only HUD or front-end polish. They want someone who can work on player-facing monetization surfaces without making them feel bolted on or incoherent with the rest of the game.

The "AAA-level UX mindset" line also implies taste and production maturity: someone who knows when to simplify, when to preserve depth, and how to design within cert, control-scheme, and implementation constraints.

#### What "great" looks like for this role

Great here looks like a designer who has already owned complex, systems-heavy game UX in shipped products, can show controller and keyboard/mouse reasoning, can create reusable component logic in Figma, and can talk credibly about tradeoffs between ideal UX and engine reality.

For this specific studio, great probably also means comfort with RPG complexity: nested systems, progression, inventory, shops, discoverability, and long-tail live-service upkeep.

#### What a hiring team is optimizing for in screening

- Clear shipped-games credibility, preferably AAA or equivalent complexity.
- Proof of owning flows, menus, systems, and not just visual polish.
- Confidence that the candidate can collaborate without hand-holding.
- Signals that the candidate can standardize patterns and leave behind reusable structure.
- Low ambiguity around seniority and domain: "this person has done serious game UX before."

#### Likely doubts unless proven otherwise

- Is the strongest proof too old relative to what the team wants now?
- Is the candidate strongest in console/controller UX, but less clearly current on dual-input gamepad plus mouse/keyboard design?
- Does the package prove expert Figma component-library ownership, or only list Figma as a tool?
- Is the commerce proof truly in-game shop UX, or partly adjacent web/storefront work?
- Is the candidate a strong systems-and-platform designer whose package is slightly broader and more eclectic than this posting wants?
