## PART 1 — What the role is really asking for

#### The explicit asks

Eleventh Hour needs a **senior game UX designer** who **owns** the **structure, flow, and usability** of player-facing **gameplay systems, menus, and commerce** on a **live, complex RPG** (Last Epoch lineage). The role is **product-minded UX**—wireframes, information architecture, **Figma** components/variables/auto layout, prototypes, and documentation that give **UI art, engineering, QA, and production** a buildable target—not UI art ownership or engineering, but with literacy for **engine, console, and cert** constraints.

Hard line items: **5+ years shipped game UX**, **strong portfolio of owned shipped UX**, **expert Figma**, **gamepad vs mouse/keyboard** fluency, **console certification** awareness, **complex RPG systems UI** and **in-game shop** experience, and **cross-functional** “definition of done” partnership. They also want someone who will **own and maintain the Figma component library**.

#### The implied asks

**Reduce ambiguity early:** dense RPG surfaces (inventory, progression, shop) need predictable patterns and IA that scale without one-off implementation thrash. **Live-service iteration** from playtests, implementation feedback, and **cert learnings** is in the same breath as Figma and flows—this is a **systems + standards** job with a **player-facing** outcome.

“AAA-level UX mindset” here reads as **taste + production tradeoffs** on **complex, shipped** work—not a literal “must have worked at AAA” badge. **ARPG** is listed under **Preferences** alongside localization-aware UI and commerce—strong fit signals, not a binary gate unless the team is unusually strict.

#### What "great" looks like for this role

A candidate who can show **receipts** for: **end-to-end ownership** of high-density game UI, **controller-first** reasoning, **monetization surfaces** that still feel native to the fantasy, **standards that survived production** (libraries, docs, handoff), and **Figma-native** or equivalent **component/variables** practice that a skeptical screener can verify in the **portfolio and artifacts**.

#### What a hiring team is optimizing for in screening

- **Fast bucket:** senior **in-game** UX, not general product design—**RPG/MMO/ARPG-ish** complexity and **shop/commerce** adjacency.
- **Proof of Figma depth** vs tool-list inflation: components, auto layout, prototypes, and **library** thinking.
- **Live title** and **implementation-close** work (playtests, cert, cross-discipline “done”) over pure concept art or marketing UI.

#### Likely doubts unless proven otherwise

- **Figma “expert”** is a high bar; if the first-read resume emphasizes **other** tools or older pipelines, screeners will hunt the portfolio for **modern Figma** or clearly transferable **component-library** practice.
- **In-house vs consulting:** some readers will ask whether recent work matches **full-time embedded** ownership and cadence on a **shipped live** RPG—fair or not, it shows up in screening.
- **ARPG** as preference: a doubt only if the package reads as **sports/linear action** only; the JD still wants **RPG systems + shop** first.
