## PART 1 — What the role is really asking for

#### The explicit asks

Riot is hiring for a senior-level game UX designer who can take messy feature space and make it buildable. The written asks are not just wireframes and mockups; they include multi-feature ownership, proof-of-concept prototyping, communication with design/engineering/art, and enough systems thinking to keep the experience coherent across touch points.

This is also clearly a craft role, not a pure facilitator role. They want someone who can produce flows, journey maps, research outputs, mockups, and interactive prototypes, then defend those decisions with player-centered rationale.

#### The implied asks

Because this is an unpublished R&D product, the team likely expects direction-setting behavior in uncertain conditions. "High-scope, complex, and often ambiguous" reads less like "make tickets cleaner" and more like "help the team discover what the product wants to be without letting UX become ornamental."

The cross-platform line matters. Even without naming every target device, Riot is signaling concern about experiential continuity between in-game and adjacent surfaces, not just one screen family.

#### What "great" looks like for this role

Great here probably looks like a senior IC who can:

- create strong interaction models early
- use prototypes to make abstract design debates concrete
- collaborate without becoming dependent on heavy process
- spot where UX standards should exist and help establish them
- keep "player-first" as an operating principle rather than a slogan

The ideal candidate likely shows both systems discipline and player empathy, with enough game literacy to make judgment calls before formal research or telemetry fully catches up.

#### What a hiring team is optimizing for in screening

- Obvious game UX credibility in under 30 seconds
- Evidence of shipped or deeply developed player-facing systems
- Clear collaboration with engineering and other disciplines
- Proof of prototyping, not just static documentation
- Signs the candidate can operate in ambiguity without becoming vague

#### Likely doubts unless proven otherwise

- Whether the candidate's strongest proof is still current enough for a 2026 senior R&D games role
- Whether the package proves 0-to-1 / incubation work, not just live-service and production hardening
- Whether multi-device fluency includes mobile, since Riot explicitly calls that out as a desired line
- Whether the candidate is a strong fit for Riot's specific player-experience-forward culture versus a more systems/platform-heavy design identity
