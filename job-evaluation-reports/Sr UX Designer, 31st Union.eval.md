## PART 1 — What the role is really asking for

#### The explicit asks
- **Senior IC UX** embedded in a game team: partner with Leads/Directors to produce **complex wireframes** quickly, then iterate flows and interaction design.
- **Diegetic UI + 3D spatial literacy**: not just flat menus—understanding UI that exists in fictional space and how layout reads in 3D contexts.
- **End-to-end ownership**: from concept through **in-engine** work; **fine-tune usability in the build**; when needed, **modify Blueprints and game data** to validate designs (Unreal-flavored toolchain assumption is explicit).
- **Visual craft**: hierarchy, contrast, graphic design principles applied to UI legibility.
- **Process + communication**: document standards/workflows; build UX artifacts (competitive analysis, personas, storyboards, journeys, wireframes, flows, prototypes); **strong writing and presentation**.
- **Tooling breadth**: Figma-class tools plus **game engines**, visual scripting, pipelines; **motion/animation is a plus**, not the core bar.

#### The implied asks
- You will be judged on whether you can **ship quality through a live pipeline**: approvals, iteration loops, cross-discipline negotiation—**without needing a producer to babysit every step**.
- **Player-first tradeoffs** under performance, input device, and readability constraints (10-foot, combat, clutter).
- **Mentorship and growth** are in the JD; they want someone who raises the UI team’s baseline, not only their own deliverables.

#### What "great" looks like for this role
- Wireframes and flows that **survive first contact with engineering**: clear states, edge cases, and implementation implications.
- Demonstrated ability to **debug feel in the build**—spacing, focus, timing, diegetic readability—not only Photoshop/Figma polish.
- **Credible Unreal workflow**: comfortable enough to **self-serve in Blueprint/data** for experiments (the JD names this outright).

#### What a hiring team is optimizing for in screening
- **Title and recency**: “Senior UX” + shipped interactive entertainment UI + engine familiarity.
- **Breadth**: systems thinking + visual craft + written specs + cross-functional stamina.
- **Unreal signal**: strong preference for candidates who won’t need months to become dangerous in-editor (even if they are not engineers).

#### Likely doubts unless proven otherwise
- **Staffing firms’ favorite failure mode**: brilliant visual portfolio, weak engine fluency—this JD explicitly tries to filter that.
- **Consultant / fractional history**: some teams worry about **velocity in a single-studio cadence** and long-horizon ownership vs hopping.
- **Domain drift**: if the resume reads **platform/identity/commerce** more than **feature UI in a current production**, a screener may mis-bucket before the portfolio saves you.
