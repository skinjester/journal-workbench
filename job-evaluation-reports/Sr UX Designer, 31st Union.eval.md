### PART 1 — What the role is really asking for

31st Union is looking for a senior game UX designer who can **own feature UX from concept through in-engine validation** and stay useful inside implementation, not just inside Figma. This is a senior IC role with mild leadership expectations, not a design manager role.

**Responsibilities behind the wording**

- Rapidly design complex feature wireframes with enough clarity to survive lead/director review and cross-functional implementation.
- Work comfortably with **diegetic UI**, **3D layouts**, and in-engine iteration.
- Fine-tune usability in the build and modify Blueprints/Game Data when needed to test ideas.
- Maintain and improve team workflows, standards, and documentation rather than only producing features.
- Mentor others opportunistically while continuing to grow personally.

**Capabilities: essential vs preferred vs implied**

- **Essential:** strong game UX craft, technical fluency, player-first judgment, engine literacy, organized communication, documentation, and production awareness.
- **Preferred / differentiators:** motion graphics and animation proficiency, especially where interface behavior and polish matter.
- **Implied:** they want someone who is senior enough to operate independently but still hands-on enough to work directly in the build and with the UI team’s production realities.

**What “great” looks like for this level/title**

- A senior IC who can think through player flow, visual hierarchy, and usability while understanding how the system actually behaves in-engine.
- Someone who can be trusted to improve workflows and documentation without turning into process-only overhead.
- A designer who can bridge UX vision and implementation details for a 3D/diegetic-heavy game.

**What a hiring team is optimizing for in screening**

- Evidence of game UX depth plus real engine fluency.
- Signals that the candidate can work on feature UX, not just menus or platform surfaces.
- Proof of strong writing, organization, and standards stewardship inside production.

**Likely doubts/risks for a candidate coming from your background unless proven otherwise**

- They explicitly want Blueprint/Game Data modification for testing. You have adjacent implementation fluency, but the exact depth may be inferred more than proven.
- Diegetic UI / 3D layouts are part of the JD’s center of gravity; you have relevant signals, but they are distributed across multiple artifacts rather than concentrated in one obvious flagship case.
- The Gamebreaking-tailored resume may read slightly over-positioned toward “lead discipline builder” versus “senior IC in-engine partner” if used without care.
