## PART 1 — What the role is really asking for

#### The explicit asks

This is a senior game UX role, not a generic product design slot. The JD wants someone who can take a messy feature from concept to in-engine implementation, make the UI intuitive and visually legible, and stay useful through leadership review and production constraints.

The obvious asks are:

- rapid wireframing for complex features
- strong judgment on 3D / diegetic UI and layout
- iteration on user flows and interaction design
- ownership through implementation, including game engine and data-tool work
- documentation that helps the team align
- mentoring and process improvement

#### The implied asks

The role is really screening for a designer who can operate inside game production, not just make screens. They want someone who understands that UX in games is shaped by controller input, spatial constraints, animation, build quality, and iteration loops with engineering and production.

The phrase "diegetic UI and 3D layouts" is the biggest tell. They want comfort with spatial thinking, not only 2D interface craft.

#### What "great" looks like for this role

Great here means the team can hand you a gnarly feature and trust you to turn it into something buildable, legible, and playable. You should be able to explain tradeoffs, prototype in the right fidelity, and keep the feature coherent as it moves through production.

Great also means you can write enough to keep the team aligned without turning UX into paperwork. The best candidate is useful in critique, useful in implementation, and useful when the feature starts to drift.

#### What a hiring team is optimizing for in screening

They are trying to answer four questions quickly:

- Can this person design for games, not just interfaces?
- Can they work with engine constraints and production reality?
- Can they communicate clearly enough that leads and directors trust the work?
- Have they actually shipped or materially shaped complex systems before?

#### Likely doubts unless proven otherwise

The main doubt is whether the candidate is current enough for a modern game team and whether the portfolio proves actual game-production depth versus retrospective storytelling.

Other doubts:

- can you do diegetic / 3D UI beyond broad claims?
- can you work inside current engine pipelines and visual scripting?
- are you a senior IC designer, or do you read more like a generalist systems designer?
- is the strongest evidence old enough to feel adjacent rather than current?
