## PART 1 — What the role is really asking for

#### The explicit asks

31st Union is asking for a senior game UX designer who can move from concept to in-engine implementation, not someone who stops at static wireframes. The explicit lines around diegetic UI, 3D layouts, Blueprints, game data, and usability tuning in build all point to a designer who is comfortable testing ideas in the medium, not only describing them.

The JD also wants a designer who can generate a lot of working artifacts quickly: wireframes, user journeys, flow diagrams, prototypes, competitive analysis, and documentation. This is a production role as much as a craft role.

#### The implied asks

The repeated emphasis on leadership approvals, team workflows, and occasional assistance from production suggests a lead-leaning senior IC. They likely want someone who can make good decisions without a lot of hand-holding, hold up under review, and leave the team with clearer methods than they found.

The phrase "player-first mindset" matters here. This is probably a team that wants interaction clarity under real gameplay conditions, not a designer who is strongest only at presentation polish.

#### What "great" looks like for this role

Great likely looks like a senior game UX designer who:

- thinks in controller ergonomics and TV-distance legibility by instinct
- can design for world-space or diegetic contexts without losing usability
- is comfortable prototyping or adjusting work inside engine
- improves team throughput through pattern libraries, reviews, and documentation
- can explain tradeoffs clearly to design, engineering, QA, and leadership

#### What a hiring team is optimizing for in screening

On first pass, they are probably optimizing for obvious shipped game UX, engine familiarity, strong interaction judgment, and evidence that the candidate has already solved controller-heavy complexity before. They will also care whether the candidate reads as current enough for an active senior production role, not just historically strong.

#### Likely doubts unless proven otherwise

- Is the strongest directly relevant game proof current enough for a present-day senior game UX hire?
- Is diegetic UI a real strength here, or only adjacent proof from prototypes and related interaction work?
- Does the candidate truly work in engine, or mostly hand off to others after concepting?
- Is the package sharply targeted to senior game UX, or does it still read as a broader systems/product/creative-tech profile with games inside it?
