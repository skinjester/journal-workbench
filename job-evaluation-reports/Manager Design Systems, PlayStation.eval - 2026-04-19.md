### PART 1 — What the role is really asking for

#### The explicit asks

PlayStation is hiring a manager who can run a real design-system program, not just contribute polished components. The role owns UI components, design tokens, documentation, governance, backlog triage, adoption, and quality across multiple consumer surfaces.

The job also explicitly asks for people management: coaching designers, supporting performance and career growth, and sustaining standards over time. This is not a pure principal IC systems role.

#### The implied asks

The team likely needs someone who can reduce entropy across many product groups without becoming a blocker. That means setting contribution rules, making prioritization tradeoffs visible, and negotiating between design-system purity and shipping pressure.

There is also an implied expectation of modern systems operations: healthy Figma libraries, token discipline, accessibility fluency, and enough product/engineering credibility to drive adoption rather than merely publish documentation.

#### What "great" looks like for this role

Great here looks like a leader who has already run a multi-product design system in production, can show component governance and adoption patterns, and has managed designers directly. The portfolio they want is not just "good UI" or "strong systems thinking"; it is proof of scalable system stewardship with organizational leverage.

#### What a hiring team is optimizing for in screening

- Fast recognition that the candidate is a design-systems leader, not a general UX lead.
- Direct proof of multi-platform component systems and governance.
- Confidence that the person can manage and coach designers, not only influence peers.
- Signals of partnership with engineering and product on roadmap, implementation friction, and adoption.
- Bonus comfort that the person understands games, platforms, and distributed teams.

#### Likely doubts unless proven otherwise

- Is this a true design-systems manager, or a strong game UX lead with some systems work?
- Has he directly managed designers in the performance/career-growth sense, or mainly led through craft and influence?
- Are tokens, Figma-library health, governance, and adoption measurement current operating strengths or mostly adjacent/inferred?
- Is accessibility a practiced strength or a missing proof point?
