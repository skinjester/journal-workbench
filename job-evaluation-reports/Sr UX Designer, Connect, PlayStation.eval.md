## PART 1 — What the role is really asking for
#### The explicit asks
- Own complex platform UX where identity, account services, safety policy, and platform technology all have to work together without confusing the player.
- Lead design across console, web, and companion surfaces, not just isolated screens.
- Translate technical and policy constraints into secure, scalable, intuitive experiences that still feel simple.
- Define reusable interaction models, navigation patterns, and system standards that other designers and teams can build on.
- Collaborate closely with product, engineering, research, trust and safety, legal, customer support, and regional partners.
- Show strong Figma-level craft plus the ability to prototype, validate, and communicate clearly.

#### The implied asks
- Be a lead IC who can create structure when the requirements are still evolving.
- Be comfortable making tradeoffs between usability, compliance, privacy, accessibility, performance, and platform consistency.
- Think in systems and journeys, not just single surfaces.
- Bring enough PlayStation or broader gaming literacy to understand how the ecosystem hangs together.
- Have proof that the candidate can make high-trust moments feel frictionless without breaking the underlying rules.

#### What "great" looks like for this role
- Someone who can take a messy account, identity, or safety problem and turn it into a coherent platform journey across multiple devices.
- A designer whose portfolio shows both craft and operational judgment: simple interaction models, good motion/structure, and decisions that hold up under scale.
- A collaborator who can move comfortably among product, engineering, research, legal, and support without losing the design point of view.
- A lead who can mentor, standardize, and raise the quality bar while still shipping.

#### What a hiring team is optimizing for in screening
- Fast bucketability as a senior platform/system designer, not a generic visual designer.
- Clear proof of shipping complex multi-surface experiences.
- Evidence that the person can work in ambiguous, technically constrained environments.
- Confidence that the candidate understands trust-sensitive experiences and can explain tradeoffs cleanly.
- A story that feels coherent for PlayStation's Connect surface rather than only adjacent to it.

#### Likely doubts unless proven otherwise
- Whether the strongest proof is older game-platform work rather than current account/safety platform work.
- Whether the public portfolio shows direct identity and safety thinking, or mainly adjacent systems and game UX.
- Whether the candidate's Figma and prototyping fluency is day-to-day practice or just a named skill.
- Whether PlayStation-specific platform knowledge is current or only inferred from prior console work.
- Assumption flag: the posting mixes core platform requirements with wishlist language common in game/AAA roles, so I treat identity/account/safety and cross-surface systems as the real must-haves and PlayStation ecosystem familiarity as a helpful bonus unless the interview says otherwise.
