## PART 1 — What the role is really asking for

#### The explicit asks

This is a senior game UX systems role for a live ARPG studio, not a general product-design opening and not a UI art seat. Eleventh Hour wants someone who can own player-facing structure across gameplay systems, menus, and in-game commerce, then turn that work into wireframes, flows, prototypes, and componentized documentation artists and engineers can build from.

The hard requirements are clear: shipped game UX, strong portfolio receipts, expert-level Figma usage, gamepad plus mouse/keyboard literacy, console-constraint awareness, complex RPG systems experience, and comfort partnering with design, UI art, engineering, QA, and production.

#### The implied asks

They are hiring for ambiguity reduction. "Versatile Figma components," "information architecture," and "definition of done" all point to a designer who creates repeatable structure early enough that engineering and art are not inventing patterns mid-production.

The commerce line matters. This is not only HUD polish or front-end menus. They want someone who can work on monetization and store-adjacent surfaces without breaking the game's broader UX logic.

#### What "great" looks like for this role

Great here looks like a senior IC who has already shipped systems-heavy game UX, can explain controller vs mouse/keyboard tradeoffs, has credible design-system habits, and can operate close to production realities rather than only making polished frames.

For this studio specifically, "great" probably also means comfort with RPG density: inventory, progression, quests, shops, discoverability, and long-tail iteration in a live product.

#### What a hiring team is optimizing for in screening

- Fast recruiter bucket: senior game UX, not generic product design.
- Proof of ownership on systems, menus, and store flows rather than only visual support work.
- Evidence that "Figma expert" is not resume inflation.
- Confidence that the candidate understands controller constraints, cert realities, and engineering handoff.

#### Likely doubts unless proven otherwise

- Whether the strongest portfolio proof is recent enough, especially on the Figma/tooling side.
- Whether MMORPG plus adjacent ARPG preproduction is close enough to a modern ARPG studio's needs.
- Whether the candidate is strongest on systems and standards but slightly lighter on explicit public commerce case-study proof than the JD suggests.
