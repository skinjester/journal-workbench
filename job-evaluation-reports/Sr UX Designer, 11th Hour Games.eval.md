### PART 1 — What the role is really asking for

#### The explicit asks

This is a senior UX ownership role for player-facing systems, not a visual UI art role. The JD wants someone who can take gameplay, menu, and commerce requirements and turn them into wireframes, flows, prototypes, component-ready Figma output, and implementation handoff that engineers and artists can actually use.

The hard asks are clear:

- shipped game UX experience, ideally at AAA or high-complexity scale
- strong Figma craft, especially components, variables, auto layout, and prototyping
- controller-first plus mouse/keyboard UX judgment
- comfort with console constraints and certification implications
- experience with complex RPG / systems-heavy gameplay UI and commerce UX
- cross-functional ownership with design, engineering, QA, and production

#### The implied asks

The studio is likely looking for someone who can reduce ambiguity, not just produce screens. This role reads like it wants a UX designer who can define structure and priorities for a fast-moving live game, keep the design system coherent, and make implementation easier instead of harder.

The role also implies a practical production mindset:

- know when a nice idea is not production-safe
- translate high-level direction into usable specs
- design for both input modes without losing clarity
- keep information architecture scalable as systems grow
- think in terms of player journeys, not isolated mockups

Assumption: in a games/AAA posting like this, the JD likely mixes must-haves and wishlist bullets. I am treating console constraints, commerce, and systems-heavy UX as core; localization and live-product exposure look strongly preferred, but not necessarily hard gates.

#### What "great" looks like for this role

Great looks like a designer who can own the end-to-end UX of a feature and make it shippable. The strongest version of this candidate would already have a pattern library mindset, know how to design for controller navigation as a first-class input, and have enough production sense to make handoff clean for engineering and content teams.

Great also means the person can talk credibly about tradeoffs:

- why a flow is structured the way it is
- how a shop or menu system survives real production constraints
- what happens when implementation, cert, or localization forces a redesign
- how to keep a player-facing system intuitive as it scales

#### What a hiring team is optimizing for in screening

Screening is probably trying to find a designer who can be trusted with complex player-facing systems without a lot of supervision. The team likely wants evidence that the candidate has already done the thing this role needs: shipped UX for games, especially around menus, commerce, and controller-first navigation.

They will likely look for:

- clear bucketability as a senior game UX designer
- proof of implementation-aware wireframes and prototypes
- evidence of console / gamepad thinking
- signs of systems thinking rather than isolated screen design
- a story that feels current enough to be credible for a live game

#### Likely doubts unless proven otherwise

The most likely questions are about recency and specificity, not whether the candidate has ever done serious work. A screener may ask:

- Does the candidate have modern Figma fluency visible in the public package?
- Is there direct proof of gamepad vs mouse/keyboard decision-making?
- How much of the strongest work is older AAA experience versus recent work?
- Is there direct evidence for RPG / commerce UX at the exact kind of complexity this role has?

Those are fair doubts for this JD, but the package does contain meaningful mitigation, especially around ESO, Madden, and the public portfolio's systems-heavy case studies.
