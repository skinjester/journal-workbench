## PART 1 — What the role is really asking for

#### The explicit asks

This is a senior game UX systems role for a live ARPG studio. The role is not UI art, brand design, or engineering. It is ownership of player-facing structure: gameplay systems, menus, shop flows, wireframes, prototypes, information architecture, and implementation-ready documentation.

The JD is explicit about Figma depth: components, variables, auto layout patterns, and prototyping. It also asks for gamepad and mouse/keyboard fluency, console constraint awareness, shipped game UX, complex RPG gameplay systems, in-game shop UX, and enough production maturity to partner with design, UI art, engineering, QA, and production.

#### The implied asks

They are hiring someone who reduces production ambiguity. "Scalable information architecture frameworks," "definition of done," and "implementation-ready experiences" all point to a designer who gives artists and engineers a stable structure before details drift.

The commerce line is not incidental. They need someone who can handle monetization-adjacent UX without treating it as a detached web checkout or pure store skin. The right candidate understands how shop, inventory, account, progression, and controller navigation affect one another.

#### What "great" looks like for this role

Great looks like a senior IC who can take a fuzzy game-system requirement and return with a clear flow, controller model, Figma prototype, componentized structure, edge-case notes, and a handoff that engineering and UI art can trust. For this studio, "great" probably also includes comfort with ARPG density: item comparison, progression, ability states, currencies, rewards, tooltips, controller focus, and repeated live updates.

#### What a hiring team is optimizing for in screening

- Fast bucket: senior game UX designer, not generic product designer.
- Direct proof of shipped game UX ownership.
- Public artifacts showing flows, systems, prototypes, and handoff thinking.
- Confidence that gamepad vs mouse/keyboard constraints are real practice, not vocabulary.
- Enough current Figma evidence to believe the candidate can own a modern component library.

#### Likely doubts unless proven otherwise

- Whether the strongest shipped RPG/system proof is recent enough.
- Whether older Fireworks/Axure-era systems work translates cleanly into Figma-native production.
- Whether ARPG adjacency through ESO/Kestrel/quest systems is close enough to Last Epoch's current needs.
- Whether commerce proof is strong enough on submittable materials, especially for in-game shop ownership.
