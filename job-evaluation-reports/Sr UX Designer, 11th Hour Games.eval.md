## PART 1 — What the role is really asking for

#### The explicit asks

- **Own end-to-end UX** for player-facing features: requirements → wireframes → flows → validation, with **Figma** prototypes and **implementation-ready** handoff (components, variables, auto layout).
- **Commerce and menus** are first-class: in-game shop experiences sit beside gameplay systems UI.
- **Input parity**: design explicitly for **gamepad and mouse/keyboard**, with awareness of **engine and console constraints**.
- **Systems weight**: complex RPG-style gameplay UI, information architecture engineers can implement and artists can skin.
- **Library ownership**: **maintain and champion** a **Figma component library** (patterns, variables, documentation).
- **Cross-functional production**: partner with design, UI art, engineering, QA, production; define “done”; iterate from feedback, playtests, implementation realities, **console certification** learnings.
- **Bar**: **5+ years** UX on **shipped games**, AAA / high-complexity preferred; **expert Figma**; portfolio proving **shipped UX ownership**.

#### The implied asks

- This is a **live ARPG studio** (Last Epoch). They want someone who can move fast, absorb production churn, and keep UX coherent across **ongoing releases**—not only a one-shot ship.
- **Monetization literacy** is in the “Preferences” list; they want shop UX that does not create cert or player-trust problems.
- **Localization-aware** UI is a preference—implies dense itemization, string length, and layout systems that do not break in translation.
- **In-engine iteration** is a preference: they want designers who do not treat the engine as someone else’s problem.

#### What "great" looks like for this role

- A portfolio and interview loop that show **repeatable ownership** of **shipped** game UX at complexity: navigation models, feature UX for dense RPG systems, and **store UX** that survives live ops.
- Demonstrated ability to **standardize** UI behavior (patterns, libraries, documentation) while **shipping**—not only concepting.
- Credible **gamepad-first** reasoning paired with PC habits; comfort discussing **cert** constraints without hand-waving.
- Evidence of **partnering** with engineering/QA to turn ambiguity into **testable** UX specs.

#### What a hiring team is optimizing for in screening

- **Fast bucket**: “Senior game UX, shipped AAA or equivalent, Figma-native, systems + shop.”
- **Risk filters**: too UI-art-forward; too web-product; too theoretical; **no live-game or commerce adjacency**; **no console** exposure.
- **Proof surface**: portfolio with **flows, prototypes, and systems thinking**; resume with **titles, shipped products, and scope** legible in seconds.

#### Likely doubts unless proven otherwise

- **Recency and context**: heavy proof from **2010s AAA** and contract studio work may raise “will they thrive in **our** toolchain and pace?” unless Figma/library ownership is made vivid.
- **Figma library owner** vs **systems designer who also uses Figma**: the JD asks for an **owner** of a living library; evidence skews toward **design systems thinking** and shipped UI—strong, but not the same keyword story as “I ran the Figma kit for X years.”
- **ARPG-specific**: strong **MMORPG + RPG systems** adjacency; direct ARPG combat HUD depth is more **implied** than documented as a multi-year focus.
- **Org fit**: posting text mixes **Eleventh Hour Games** studio voice with **KRAFTON Americas** legal/footer—candidates should expect **matrixed/publisher** norms even if day-to-day is small-team.
