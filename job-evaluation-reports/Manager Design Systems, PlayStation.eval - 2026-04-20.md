### PART 1 — What the role is really asking for

#### The explicit asks

This is a design-systems management role, not a general senior UX role with some systems exposure. PlayStation is asking for someone who can run the operating system around the system: components, tokens, documentation, contribution rules, backlog triage, design debt, and adoption across multiple consumer products.

It is also explicitly a people-management job. The posting names coaching, development, performance, accountability, and culture building. That is not a nice-to-have hidden in vague leadership language.

#### The implied asks

The team likely needs someone who can reduce entropy across many product teams without turning the system into a bottleneck. That means making governance usable, balancing long-term integrity against local shipping pressure, and earning adoption from engineering and product rather than relying on authority alone.

There is also an implied expectation of modern systems stewardship: healthy Figma libraries, token discipline, accessibility fluency, and roadmap judgment. The posting reads like they want someone who has already run this machinery in production, not someone who can probably figure it out.

#### What "great" looks like for this role

Great looks like a manager who can show all of the following in public materials: multi-platform system ownership, current component and token practice, governance and contribution flows, partnership with engineering and product on prioritization, and direct people-management credibility. Bonus game experience helps, but it does not replace the manager/design-systems core.

#### What a hiring team is optimizing for in screening

- Fast recognition that this is a design-systems manager, not a strong UX lead trying to stretch into the role.
- Public proof of reusable components, system governance, and multi-team adoption.
- Confidence that the candidate has directly managed designers, not only mentored peers.
- Signals that engineering partnership, prioritization, and production readiness are practiced strengths.
- Comfort with consumer gaming products and distributed collaboration.

#### Likely doubts unless proven otherwise

- Is this a real design-systems manager or a strong systems-minded lead IC?
- Is the management claim formal enough for performance and career-growth ownership?
- Are tokens, governance, and Figma-library stewardship current operating strengths or mostly adjacent?
- Is accessibility expertise real and practiced, or just assumed baseline competence?
- Is the strongest systems proof too dated relative to what this team needs now?
