## PART 1 — What the role is really asking for

#### The explicit asks

- A senior UX designer for streamer-creation and viewer-engagement work across Twitch's cross-platform product ecosystem.
- Strong interaction design and visual craft, including mobile prototyping.
- Hands-on research and close partnership with product, engineering, and design.
- Program-level leadership: senior XFN collaboration, executive presentation, and mentorship of junior designers.
- Ability to combine qualitative and quantitative inputs into hypotheses and product direction.
- Figma fluency is assumed baseline tooling.

#### The implied asks

- This is not a pure feature-delivery IC role. They want someone who can give structure to a broad, ongoing product area.
- "Content discovery and/or content engagement" reads like a real filter, not boilerplate. I would treat it as core unless the hiring team proves otherwise.
- They likely want consumer-product instincts around creator behavior, audience behavior, and repeat engagement loops, not just good interface craft.
- Because the team spans streamer and viewer experiences, they are probably screening for someone who can think in ecosystems, not isolated screens.
- The mention of executive visibility and mentoring suggests they want a senior IC who can stabilize design quality around them.

#### What "great" looks like for this role

- Someone whose portfolio already shows engagement, creation, or community-adjacent product problems, ideally with clear behavior change or product outcomes.
- A designer who can move between concepting, prototyping, research synthesis, and shipping detail without losing clarity.
- A candidate who can talk credibly about customer motivations, not just UI mechanics.
- A package that reads as "senior consumer/community UX in gaming or adjacent internet product," not merely "experienced game UI designer."

#### What a hiring team is optimizing for in screening

- Fast bucketability into senior gaming/community/product UX.
- Direct proof of content engagement or discovery work.
- Confidence the candidate can influence roadmap and represent design to senior stakeholders.
- Evidence of research-backed decision making and strong execution craft.
- Low ambiguity about tools and surface area, especially mobile and Figma.

#### Likely doubts unless proven otherwise

- Whether a games-heavy background translates to creator/community product work rather than game UI or service UX.
- Whether the candidate has real viewer/streamer empathy or UGC-platform intuition, versus adjacent live-service experience.
- Whether the strongest proof is current enough, and public enough, for a modern senior consumer-product screen.
- Whether mobile interaction work, quant-informed product thinking, and roadmap impact are actually part of the package or only inferred.
