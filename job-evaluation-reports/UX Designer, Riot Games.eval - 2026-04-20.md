### PART 1 — What the role is really asking for

#### The explicit asks

Riot is hiring a senior game UX designer who can work across complex feature space, not a narrow production-line UI executor. The role asks for someone who can design player-facing flows from concept through development, produce wireframes and interactive prototypes, communicate rationale across design/engineering/art, and help establish UX standards in an ambiguous R&D setting.

The hard requirements are intentionally broad: games or digital-product UX experience, a credible portfolio, proficiency with common design/prototyping tools, and enough craft range to move between strategy articulation, research, flows, wireframes, mockups, and prototypes. The title says UX Designer III, but the description reads like a senior IC who can influence beyond their own ticket queue.

#### The implied asks

- They want someone comfortable shaping a product before the product is fully defined.
- They want a designer who can survive ambiguity without becoming precious about process.
- They want cross-functional trust: someone design, engineering, and art will listen to.
- They likely value systems thinking more than polished mockups alone.
- They would prefer someone with genuine games intuition, not a generalist trying to translate enterprise UX language into games after the fact.

#### What "great" looks like for this role

Great here probably means a designer who can take messy mechanics or feature areas, turn them into legible player journeys, prototype enough to de-risk direction, and keep the experience coherent across surfaces and handoffs. Because this is unpublished R&D, "great" also includes judgment: knowing when to stay abstract, when to prototype, and when to formalize patterns or standards for the rest of the team.

#### What a hiring team is optimizing for in screening

- Clear evidence of shipped or deeply developed game UX work.
- Proof of interaction design depth, not just visual polish.
- Comfort with prototyping and concept development.
- Signs of leadership through reviews, systems, frameworks, or standards.
- A portfolio that makes player-facing problem solving obvious fast.

#### Likely doubts unless proven otherwise

- How current is the strongest game proof relative to a present-day senior R&D role?
- Is the candidate strongest in console/system UX, or equally credible on broader multi-platform product UX?
- How much hands-on feature ownership existed in ambiguous preproduction versus polishing established live-service surfaces?
- Can they credibly support mobile or cross-device asks, which Riot names as a desired area?
- Does the package tell a Riot-relevant story, or just a generally strong games UX story?
