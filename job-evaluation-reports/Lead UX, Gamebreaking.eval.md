### PART 1 — What the role is really asking for

This is not a generic senior UX posting. Gamebreaking wants a **foundational hire** who can act like the first real UX owner inside a small co-dev studio, embed with a large AAA client, and push standards, documentation, wireframes, prototypes, and scalable UI without much supervision.

**Responsibilities behind the wording**

- Be the first durable UX point of view at the studio, not just another hands-on designer.
- Work embedded with a client team while still raising the bar internally at Gamebreaking.
- Move quickly from complex wireframes to prototyping to implementation-facing scalable UI.
- Establish standards for speed and quality, keep documentation alive, and improve tools/methods.
- Contribute to culture and feedback quality, which means they want someone who can coach and influence, not just ship their own work.

**Capabilities: essential vs preferred vs implied**

- **Essential:** strong game UX craft, proven collaboration, leadership by influence, comfort in large organizations, ownership without oversight, scalable UI thinking, prototyping.
- **Preferred / differentiators:** Unreal Engine with **UMG**, material-based motion design, diegetic UI and 3D layouts, motion graphics, broad visual/prototype tool fluency.
- **Implied:** they want a designer who can be both a **lead IC** and a discipline builder inside a still-growing studio structure.

**What “great” looks like for this level/title**

- Someone who can join an existing AAA production, understand its current UX patterns quickly, and improve them without creating political drag.
- A designer who produces standards and documentation other people actually use.
- A lead who can coach peers, give critique well, and create momentum where UX maturity is still developing.

**What a hiring team is optimizing for in screening**

- Proof of senior game UX credibility, especially in complex systems and production realities.
- Evidence of tools/process ownership, not just screen craft.
- Confidence that the person can operate as a **first / lead UX presence** on a small team and not need hand-holding.

**Likely doubts/risks for a candidate coming from your background unless proven otherwise**

- The title fit is mostly good, but they may ask whether you want to be a **hands-on embedded lead** versus a broader principal/consultant profile.
- They explicitly call out **Unreal UMG**, diegetic UI, and 3D layouts. You have adjacent proof, but not all of it is obvious from the default materials.
- Because this is a co-dev environment, they may wonder how well your consulting/contract work maps to being the first UX function in a small remote studio.

- It does not make **UMG** and material-based motion design as explicit as the JD would ideally like.

- Some of the best proof is older shipped work, so the recency story still depends on how the recent R&D and prototype material is framed.

#### Hiring risks the portfolio reduces vs leaves open

- **Reduces:** risk that you cannot establish patterns and scalable UI language.
- **Reduces:** risk that you are only a service/platform UX designer.
- **Reduces:** risk that you cannot work close to implementation and prototype behavior.
- **Leaves open:** how much direct people leadership/coaching you want to do day to day.
- **Leaves open:** whether your Unreal/UMG depth is strong enough for a team that wants that toolchain daily.

#### Credibility and receipts

The strongest portfolio proof is credible because it is grounded in specific constraints, artifacts, and interaction problems. The weaker area is broad headline marketing language or large impact framing, which is less useful than the case-study mechanics themselves. For this JD, the best receipts are the systems, patterns, and prototyping details, not the headline claims.
