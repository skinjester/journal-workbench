## PART 1 — What the role is really asking for
#### The explicit asks
- First UX hire for a growing game studio working on a large, long-running co-dev project.
- Own complex UX problems end to end: wireframes, prototyping, standards, documentation, and iteration with leadership/production.
- Design for console/gamepad-first play inside an existing AAA collaboration, not a blank-slate greenfield toy.
- Be constructive with feedback, coach other designers, and help set a positive team culture.
- Bring game UX depth, plus enough flexibility to contribute outside a narrow silo.

#### The implied asks
- A senior designer who can walk into ambiguity without supervision and create order fast.
- Someone who can argue for better UX standards without sounding academic or brittle.
- Proven ability to work across design, engineering, production, and external partners.
- Comfort with game implementation constraints, especially controller flows, layout systems, and prototyping.
- Enough technical fluency to collaborate on Unreal/UI tooling and spatial or motion-heavy presentation.

#### What "great" looks like for this role
- A veteran game UX lead who can make a messy feature legible in days, not weeks.
- Someone who can set a bar for speed and quality without becoming a bottleneck.
- A designer whose portfolio shows shipped or near-shipped systems, not just polished concepts.
- Clear evidence of cross-discipline leadership and practical collaboration, especially in live or embedded teams.
- Bonus points if they have modern tooling fluency, spatial UI instincts, and public proof of teaching or mentoring.

#### What a hiring team is optimizing for in screening
- Can this person immediately make our game easier to use on controller and keep the team moving?
- Will they fit inside a production-heavy org without needing constant direction?
- Do they sound like a real game UX lead, or a generalist designer with game-adjacent taste?
- Can they explain systems, tradeoffs, and implementation details without hand-waving?
- Are they credible enough to lead a first UX discipline at a studio that wants to grow it?

#### Likely doubts unless proven otherwise
- The strongest public shipping proof is older; the role is current, so recency needs to be inferred carefully.
- The JD explicitly names Unreal UMG and material-based motion design; the public package does not prove those directly.
- First-UX-hire leadership sounds plausible, but the package does not show a lot of modern team-scale management detail.
- The site mixes game UX with creative-tech/art-installation work, which can blur the recruiter bucket.
- If they want a pure AAA live-service UX specialist, they may still ask for more recent, game-only evidence.
