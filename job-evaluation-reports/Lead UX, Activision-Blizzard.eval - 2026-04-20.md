# PART 1 — What the role is really asking for

#### The explicit asks

This is not a generic UX lead posting. It is a lead-level game UX role embedded in the `Call of Duty: Mobile` product team, expected to convert player insights, data, and research into concepts, flows, prototypes, and high-level design direction. The role is explicitly mobile-first, explicitly game-focused, and explicitly cross-functional with PMs plus an external development partner at Tencent.

The hiring team also wants someone who can establish UX standards and process because this is the first dedicated UX role on the team. That means the job is not just about designing features. It is about creating the operating model for UX inside a product organization that already ships live content.

#### The implied asks

They are likely trying to hire someone who can sit between product strategy and detailed production design: identify where UX changes will move player outcomes, frame those opportunities in a way PMs can prioritize, and give Tencent a strong enough direction that implementation quality does not drift.

Because the JD calls out postlaunch evaluation, player data, competitor analysis, and high-impact opportunities, this reads as a product-UX leadership role inside a live-service business, not a pure feature-wireframing role. The mobile/F2P context implies sensitivity to retention, monetization friction, readability on small screens, and event/live-ops cadence even though the JD does not spell each of those out.

#### What "great" looks like for this role

Great likely looks like a senior game UX lead who has already worked on mobile or F2P live-service products, can speak both player empathy and business/product language, and can influence an external partner without needing to own every pixel personally. They would show recent examples of identifying a player problem, framing it with data or research, shipping a solution, and then measuring the result after launch.

#### What a hiring team is optimizing for in screening

In screening, they are probably optimizing for five things:

- obvious game UX credibility at lead level
- mobile-first judgment, ideally in shipped F2P/live-service work
- product-mindedness, not only craft
- enough systems thinking to shape standards/process from scratch
- collaboration maturity for PM partnership and Tencent coordination

#### Likely doubts unless proven otherwise

A skeptical screener will look for gaps around direct mobile game experience, F2P/live-ops proof, player-data-driven iteration, and recent evidence of leading UX direction rather than only designing interfaces. They may also wonder whether the candidate is strongest in console/front-end systems rather than mobile product UX, and whether the package shows enough evidence of working through an external development partner model.
