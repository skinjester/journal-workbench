## PART 1 — What the role is really asking for

#### The explicit asks

Riot is hiring a senior game UX designer for an unpublished R&D product who can own complex player-facing problem areas, not just polish screens. The JD is explicit about wireframes, interactive prototypes, presentations, research/discovery artifacts, and design rationale that can survive cross-functional scrutiny.

The role also expects someone who can span concept through development, collaborate tightly with design, engineering, and art, and help set best practices and quality standards. This is not a narrow production-wireframing role.

#### The implied asks

The strongest implied ask is ambiguity handling. "High-scope, complex, and often ambiguous" plus "multiple features" and "span across teams" means Riot wants someone who can impose structure before requirements are stable.

This also reads like a hybrid systems/interaction designer rather than a pure visual UI specialist or pure researcher. The desired qualifications point to breadth: information architecture, prototyping, research, visual design, and cross-device thinking.

Because the product is unpublished R&D, internal persuasion matters almost as much as craft. The team likely needs someone who can make future-facing work feel concrete enough for design leadership, engineering, and adjacent designers to react to.

#### What "great" looks like for this role

Great here looks like a designer who can turn fuzzy mechanics or feature concepts into legible player journeys, prototype the risky parts early, and create reusable patterns the team can build from. The ideal candidate probably has enough game literacy to make controls, pacing, and readability feel native rather than imported from general product UX.

At this level, "great" also means improving team clarity: better critique, clearer artifacts, better standards, fewer downstream misunderstandings.

#### What a hiring team is optimizing for in screening

- Visible game UX credibility in the first pass.
- Portfolio proof of systems thinking, interaction design, and feature architecture.
- Evidence of collaborative range across design, engineering, art, and implementation.
- Signs that the candidate can work in preproduction or R&D ambiguity without drifting into vague strategy language.
- A package that feels player-experience-first, not merely platform-UX adjacent.

#### Likely doubts unless proven otherwise

- How recent is the strongest shipped AAA game proof?
- Is there real evidence of multi-feature R&D work, or mostly one major legacy title plus adjacent consulting?
- Where is the visible proof for mobile game UX or broader device/form-factor fluency?
- Does the package show a Riot-style player-experience designer, or a systems-heavy game UX generalist who could still read slightly sideways to this team?
