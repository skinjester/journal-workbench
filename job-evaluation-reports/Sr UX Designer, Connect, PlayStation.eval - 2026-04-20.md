# PART 1 — What the role is really asking for

#### The explicit asks

This is a senior platform-systems UX role inside PlayStation, not a feature-level game UX role. The JD is asking for someone who can own a core player journey or platform surface across console, web, and companion contexts, and do it in domains where mistakes create trust, compliance, and support costs.

The named domains matter: accounts, identity, safety, self-service/support, family-management-adjacent experiences, and platform standards. The team wants someone who can turn policy, platform, and technical constraints into usable interaction models, not just make polished screens.

The craft bar is also real. They explicitly want strong interaction design, scalable UI frameworks, prototyping, presentation clarity, and modern Figma fluency. This is not just systems strategy; it is systems strategy plus hands-on execution.

#### The implied asks

- A designer who can work in messy organizational seams: Product, Engineering, Trust and Safety, Legal, Customer Support, and regional teams.
- Someone who can define patterns and standards that other teams will reuse, not just ship one-off flows.
- A designer comfortable with sensitive tradeoffs where usability, privacy, compliance, localization, and business needs conflict.
- A person who can make platform work feel coherent across surfaces and regions, which usually means strong information architecture and governance instincts.

#### What "great" looks like for this role

Great here likely means:

- account and identity flows that feel low-friction but trustworthy
- standards and interaction patterns that scale across surfaces
- enough systems thinking to reduce future UX debt, not just solve the current ticket
- strong critique and mentorship presence without becoming process-heavy
- credible collaboration with legal/support/trust stakeholders, where clarity matters as much as craft

#### What a hiring team is optimizing for in screening

On first pass, they are probably screening for a candidate who can be bucketed quickly as a senior platform/product designer with cross-surface systems experience, not merely a gameplay or visual UI designer.

They will also be looking for direct public proof of:

- account or identity-adjacent work
- complex multi-surface shipping
- scalable design systems/standards
- strong interaction design
- enough business/operations maturity to handle safety, privacy, compliance, and regional complexity

#### Likely doubts unless proven otherwise

- Is the background too game-UX-heavy versus trust/platform-service-heavy?
- Where is the direct public proof for safety, privacy, legal, and family-management work?
- Is the strongest relevant proof recent enough, or is the best match mostly older console/web work?
- How much modern Figma systems fluency is visible beyond a keyword line?
- Are mobile/companion and globally localized service experiences directly proven, or mostly adjacent/inferred?
