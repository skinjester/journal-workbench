### PART 1 — What the role is really asking for

This is not a generic senior UX posting. It is a game-specific lead role for a new multiplayer product where CDPR wants someone who can own player-facing interaction quality, set UX direction with a UI art counterpart, and keep that direction practical enough to survive production.

#### The explicit asks

- Lead UX for UI layouts, navigation, menus, and end-to-end player journeys.
- Partner tightly with the Lead UI Artist, UX Research, Production, and broader design.
- Guide a team of UX designers and establish methods, standards, and inclusive best practices.
- Prototype layouts and interaction systems.
- Use playtests, research, marketing insight, and community feedback to refine the experience.
- Maintain documentation and keep implementation aligned with timelines and resources.
- Bring at least one shipped AAA title and at least some prior leadership experience.

#### The implied asks

- They need someone who can turn a messy multiplayer RPG feature set into a readable, controller-friendly, progression-friendly experience.
- Because this is a new role on a multiplayer Witcher project, they likely want a lead who can create UX structure before the org fully settles around it.
- The UX lead here is probably expected to be opinionated enough to drive standards, but pragmatic enough to work through UI art, design, engineering, and production constraints rather than throwing idealized flows over the wall.
- The JD says "interest in implementing and refining UX features in Unreal Engine." I read that as a meaningful plus, not necessarily a hard requirement for deep Blueprint production ownership, unless the studio is quietly expecting a very hands-on proto lead.

#### What "great" looks like for this role

- A candidate who has already shipped large-scale game UX, preferably multiplayer or live-service adjacent, and can show how they translated complexity into intuitive player journeys.
- Someone who can articulate a UX point of view at system level: navigation philosophy, information architecture, interaction patterns, accessibility, moment-to-moment clarity.
- A lead who has influenced other designers and adjacent functions, not just produced flows alone.
- A portfolio that shows both concept development and the less glamorous production backbone: audits, guidelines, documentation, playtest iteration, and implementation tradeoffs.

#### What a hiring team is optimizing for in screening

- Immediate proof of AAA game credibility.
- Confidence that the candidate can lead UX direction rather than just contribute screens.
- Evidence of multiplayer or live-service complexity, since Project Sirius is multiplayer even though multiplayer experience is technically listed as a nice-to-have.
- Enough craft and prototyping fluency to collaborate credibly with UI art and implementation partners.
- A clean application story that says, quickly, "this person has done lead-level game UX on complex shipped products before."

#### Likely doubts unless proven otherwise

- How recent is the strongest shipped AAA game proof, and has the candidate stayed close enough to modern production realities?
- Has he actually led people and methods, or mostly led through craft and influence?
- Is the strongest public proof weighted toward systems and front-end architecture more than toward current-generation player journey leadership on a new multiplayer title?
- How hands-on is he in Unreal today versus using it selectively for prototypes?
- Assumption to flag: CDPR's "shipped at least one AAA title" reads like a real must-have. "Multiplayer" is labeled nice-to-have, but given the project context it likely matters more than boilerplate. Unreal likely matters, but probably below shipped AAA and leadership in screening order.
