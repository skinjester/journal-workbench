## PART 1 — What the role is really asking for

#### The explicit asks

- Own the UX direction for a new multiplayer RPG.
- Collaborate tightly with UI art, researchers, production, and the broader design team.
- Lead a small UX team and establish inclusive best practices.
- Prototype and refine layouts, navigation, and usability systems.
- Translate playtests and research into concrete design changes.
- Produce durable documentation and keep implementation aligned.

#### The implied asks

- Be the person who turns fuzzy game direction into a navigable, shippable interface strategy.
- Reduce friction in controller-first navigation, inventory, menus, combat-adjacent flows, and player journeys.
- Bring enough experience to make tradeoffs quickly without needing constant senior rescue.
- Handle cross-department politics without making the team feel managed into a corner.
- Be current enough on game UX conventions to talk credibly about modern multiplayer expectations.

#### What "great" looks like for this role

- A lead who can frame the UX problem, not just mock screens.
- Strong console and information architecture instincts.
- Evidence of playtest-informed iteration, not purely aesthetic design.
- Comfortable in a live production environment where implementation, timing, and team alignment matter.
- Able to guide others through standards, critique, and documentation.

#### What a hiring team is optimizing for in screening

- Immediate fit for a senior game UX lead, not a general product designer.
- Proof of shipped AAA game work.
- Evidence of multiplayer, controller, and system-heavy UX.
- Clarity that the candidate can influence cross-functionally and communicate cleanly.
- Confidence that the person can lead a team without creating avoidable process drag.

#### Likely doubts unless proven otherwise

- Whether the candidate is current enough for a 2026 multiplayer project.
- Whether the leadership is formal team leadership or mostly influence-through-craft.
- Whether the public package proves modern Unreal or current live-service collaboration.
- Whether the candidate reads as a game UX leader first, not as a legacy systems designer.
