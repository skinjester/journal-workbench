## PART 1 — What the role is really asking for
#### The explicit asks
- Define the end-to-end UX of a multiplayer RPG: menus, navigation, player journeys, interaction rules, and clarity under live gameplay constraints.
- Lead UX design across departments: UI art, UX research, production, engineering, and design leadership.
- Establish inclusive practices, documentation, and a repeatable methodology that other designers can use.
- Prototype, run playtests, and translate feedback into improved flow and interaction behavior.
- Ship in Unreal-adjacent game workflows, with standard prototyping and collaboration tools.

#### The implied asks
- Be a strong lead IC who can set direction without waiting for perfect requirements.
- Be comfortable with ambiguity and evolving game design, especially in early production.
- Make tradeoffs between player clarity, production reality, and visual/world fidelity.
- Communicate clearly enough that UI, research, engineering, and production can all use the same design story.
- Read as someone who has already shipped at least one AAA game and knows how game UX differs from generic product UX.

#### What "great" looks like for this role
- Someone who can own the UX system for a live or live-like game, not just isolated screens.
- A designer who can turn playtest observations into practical menu, navigation, and interaction changes.
- A lead who can raise the quality bar for a team through standards, critique, documentation, and concrete prototypes.
- A candidate whose portfolio proves both craft and shipped accountability, not just polished visuals.

#### What a hiring team is optimizing for in screening
- Fast confirmation of shipped AAA game UX experience.
- Evidence of multiplayer, controller-first, or console-native thinking.
- Evidence that the person can lead cross-functional collaboration without becoming a bottleneck.
- Proof that the candidate can prototype and iterate with the team, not merely hand off concepts.
- A story that feels current enough for a new role, even if the strongest ship proof is older.

#### Likely doubts unless proven otherwise
- Whether the candidate has current enough game UX exposure for a new AAA multiplayer title.
- Whether the leadership here is “lead designer” leadership or mostly senior IC influence.
- Whether Unreal is a real working skill or just a mention in a side project.
- Whether the Boston hybrid requirement is a practical fit.
- Whether the public package shows enough current game taste and current industry awareness.
